## Research: AI Agents in Insurance & Actuarial Science

### Overview
Insurance is fundamentally an information processing business — assessing risk, pricing it, and paying claims. AI agents are transforming every part of the insurance value chain, from underwriting to claims processing to fraud detection. The industry processes trillions of dollars in premiums annually, making it a massive opportunity for AI-driven efficiency.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities in Insurance

**1. Underwriting Automation**
- AI reviewing applicant data and generating risk assessments
- Natural language processing of medical records, driving records, credit reports
- Property inspection via satellite imagery and drones
- Life expectancy modeling for life insurance
- Workers' comp risk assessment for businesses
- Dynamic pricing: AI adjusting premiums in real-time based on behavior (telematics)

**2. Claims Processing & Automation**
- AI first-pass claims review for fast-track approval
- Damage assessment via computer vision (auto, home, crop)
- FNOL (First Notice of Loss) automation via conversational AI
- Coverage determination: AI checks policy language vs claim facts
- Subrogation: AI identifies third-party liability
- Claims triage: routing complex claims to adjusters

**3. Fraud Detection**
- AI pattern recognition across claims data
- Network analysis: detecting fraud rings
- Behavioral biometrics for identity verification
- Anomaly detection in claims patterns
- Real-time fraud scoring at point of claim
- NCIA + insurance industry partnerships

**4. Actuarial Modeling**
- AI building mortality/morbidity tables
- Catastrophe modeling (hurricanes, earthquakes)
- Climate change risk adjustments
- Claims frequency and severity prediction
- Reserve sufficiency analysis
- AI scenario planning for extreme events

**5. Customer Experience & Distribution**
- AI chatbots for policy quotes and servicing
- Personalized insurance product recommendations
- AI agents that manage insurance portfolios on behalf of customers
- Parametric insurance products triggered automatically by events
- AI for broker/advisor support tools

### Challenges
1. **Explainability** — insurers must explain why they denied coverage
2. **Fairness/bias** — AI may discriminate in pricing (though often less than human underwriters)
3. **Regulatory complexity** — state-by-state insurance regulation
4. **Legacy systems** — mainframes from the 1970s still running
5. **Data quality** — actuarial data is often incomplete and inconsistent
6. **Adversarial fraud** — sophisticated fraudsters will adapt to AI

### Future Trajectory (2026-2030)
- Autonomous insurance: claims paid within minutes of incident
- Hyper-personalized insurance: pricing updates daily based on behavior
- Parametric insurance: payouts triggered automatically by sensors
- AI agents that negotiate insurance on your behalf
- Preventative AI: AI that prevents accidents before they happen
- Sovereign identity + insurance: blockchain-based verified credentials
