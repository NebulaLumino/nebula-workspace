## Research: AI Agents in International AI Governance, Treaties & Arms Control

### Overview
AI is a genuinely global technology, but governance is fragmented across jurisdictions. As AI becomes more capable and autonomous, international coordination on AI safety, military applications, and economic competition is becoming urgent — with some comparing the moment to the early nuclear age.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities in AI Governance

**1. AI Arms Control & Verification**
- AI that monitors compliance with AI weapons treaties
- Lethal Autonomous Weapon Systems (LAWS) detection and tracking
- Dual-use AI export control compliance monitoring
- AI for verifying arms control agreements (e.g., nuclear test ban monitoring)
- Autonomous military system behavior logging and auditing

**2. International AI Standards Bodies**
- ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 42 — international AI standards
- OECD AI Policy Observatory — tracking AI policies worldwide
- NIST (US) — AI standards and frameworks
- IEEE — technical standards for AI
- UN AI Advisory Body (2024) — UN's first AI governance body

**3. AI Treaty Drafting & Negotiation Support**
- AI that models economic and security effects of proposed AI regulations
- Comparative AI law analysis (what each country is doing)
- Trade agreement AI provisions negotiation support
- Sanctions compliance AI for AI-related transfers
- AI intellectual property framework negotiation support

**4. AI Economic Competition & Trade**
- AI monitoring of strategic technology competition
- Export control compliance AI (BIS Entity List, EAR)
- Antitrust AI for global market analysis
- Technology transfer monitoring AI
- AI supply chain security assessment

**5. AI Safety & Research Governance**
- AI labs safety commitment monitoring (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind)
- Compute governance (who can train the largest models?)
- Data governance for AI training (what data can be used?)
- AI incident reporting database (similar to aviation near-misses)
- International AI safety research coordination

### Current Governance Landscape
- **EU AI Act (2024)** — most comprehensive AI regulation globally
- **US Executive Order on AI (2023)** — framework for AI safety and security
- **UK AI Safety Institute (2023)** — world's first government AI safety body
- **Bletchley Declaration (2023)** — UK + US + EU + others on AI existential risk
- **Seoul AI Safety Commitments (2024)** — frontier AI labs' safety commitments
- **UN AI Advisory Body (2024)** — developing global AI governance framework

### Military AI & LAWS
- **Lethal Autonomous Weapons Systems (LAWS)** — weapons that select and engage targets without human oversight
- **Campaign to Stop Killer Robots** — coalition calling for preemptive ban
- **US DoD Directive 3000.09** — requires human oversight of autonomous weapons
- **UN CCW discussions** — ongoing UN debate on LAWS under Convention on Certain Conventional Weapons

### Challenges
1. **Verification** — AI capabilities are hard to verify (vs. nuclear warheads)
2. **Dual use** — most AI has both civilian and military applications
3. **Competitiveness vs. safety** — countries may resist safety regulations to stay competitive
4. **Governance gap** — AI is moving faster than governance institutions
5. **Enforcement** — no global enforcement mechanism for AI rules

### Future Trajectory (2026-2030)
- First international AI treaty (possibly on LAWS or frontier AI safety)
- Global AI incident database with mandatory reporting
- Compute threshold for safety evaluations (any model above X must be evaluated)
- International AI Safety Institute network (like IAEA for AI)
- Arms control for AI in nuclear command-and-control
