## Research: AI Agents in Cyber-Physical System Security & Critical Infrastructure Resilience

### Overview
AI agents increasingly control critical infrastructure — power grids, water treatment plants, nuclear facilities, hospitals, traffic systems, and factories. When these AI systems are hacked or malfunction, the consequences can be physical, not just digital. This convergence of cybersecurity and physical safety is one of the most consequential AI risk domains.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities in CPS Security

**1. Industrial Control System (ICS) / SCADA AI**
- Anomaly detection in industrial control systems
- Predictive maintenance for physical infrastructure
- SCADA network monitoring AI
- PLC (Programmable Logic Controller) behavior monitoring
- Industrial protocol analysis (Modbus, DNP3, Profinet)

**2. AI-Powered Cyber-Physical Attack Detection**
- AI that detects Stuxnet-style attacks (targeting centrifuges via control system)
- Intrusion detection for IoT and connected devices
- False data injection attack detection (adversarial data manipulation)
- Supply chain attack detection in hardware/software
- Zero-day exploit pre-detection using behavioral analysis

**3. Autonomous Grid & Infrastructure AI**
- AI-controlled power grid management (often called "self-healing grids")
- AI for power dispatch, load balancing, renewable integration
- Water treatment plant AI optimization
- Gas pipeline AI monitoring and anomaly detection
- Traffic signal AI (adaptive signal control — often city-scale)

**4. Resilience & Recovery AI**
- Digital twin-based infrastructure simulation and attack scenario modeling
- AI-driven incident response for physical infrastructure
- Automated failover and system restoration
- Cascading failure prediction (power → water → hospital → etc.)
- AI war-gaming for critical infrastructure protection

**5. AI as Attack Vector in CPS**
- AI-generated malware targeting specific industrial systems
- AI-assisted spear-phishing for OT (operational technology) environments
- Adversarial AI against autonomous vehicles (adversarial patches, signal manipulation)
- AI-powered social engineering for physical access (deepfake executives)
- AI that learns and mimics industrial control patterns to blend in

### Notable Incidents & Case Studies
- **Stuxnet (2010)** — US/Israeli cyberweapon destroying Iranian centrifuges (first known CPS attack)
- **Colonial Pipeline (2021)** — ransomware shutting down US East Coast fuel pipeline
- **Triton/TRITON (2017)** — malware targeting safety systems in petrochemical plants
- **Ukrainian power grid attacks (2015, 2016)** — first confirmed grid cyberattacks
- **Oldsmar water utility attack (2021)** — hacker tried to poison water supply via SCADA
- **Shanghai port attack (2021)** — serious ransomware affecting global shipping

### Key Frameworks & Standards
- **NIST Cybersecurity Framework** — critical infrastructure cybersecurity
- **IEC 62443** — industrial automation and control systems security
- **CISA (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency)** — US critical infrastructure protection
- **NSA/CSS Cybersecurity** — national security systems guidance
- **EU NIS2 Directive** — network and information systems security for critical infrastructure

### Defensive AI Tools
- **Claroty** — industrial network detection and response
- **Dragos** — ICS/OT cybersecurity specifically
- **Nozomi Networks** — OT/IoT security and visibility
- **Armis** — agentless device security
- **X-Force** (IBM) — industrial control systems threat intelligence

### Challenges
1. **Air-gapped systems** — can't update or monitor systems that aren't connected
2. **Legacy systems** — old PLCs never designed with security in mind
3. **Availability vs. security** — systems must be always-on but also secure
4. **OT/IT convergence** — connecting IT and OT networks creates new attack surfaces
5. **Attribution** — attackers in critical infrastructure are often nation-states

### Future Trajectory (2026-2030)
- AI-powered autonomous grid management (with AI keeping the grid stable)
- "Self-healing infrastructure" — AI that detects and repairs its own attacks
- AI-red team for critical infrastructure (AI attacks infrastructure to find vulnerabilities)
- International norms against AI attacks on critical infrastructure (like nuclear norms)
- AI in nuclear command-and-control — safety vs. security paradox
