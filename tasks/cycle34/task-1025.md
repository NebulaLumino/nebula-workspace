## Research: AI Agents in Universal Basic Income, Wealth Redistribution & Post-Labor Economics

### Overview
As AI automation accelerates, economists, philosophers, and policymakers are grappling with how economic value created by AI should be distributed. UBI, robot taxes, wealth funds, and "post-labor" economics are moving from fringe ideas to mainstream policy debate.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities in Economic Distribution

**1. AI-Driven Economic Modeling for UBI**
- Microsimulation models that predict UBI impacts on poverty, labor supply, inequality
- Optimal UBI level determination (AI that models which amount maximizes welfare)
- Dynamic modeling of UBI's effects on health, education, entrepreneurship
- Cross-country comparison analysis
- AI that identifies which welfare programs UBI would replace or supplement

**2. Automated Wealth Taxation & Redistribution AI**
- Wealth tax compliance monitoring (tracking assets across borders)
- Automated tax collection from AI-generated economic activity
- International tax coordination AI (addressing tax haven problems)
- Carbon border adjustment mechanism AI (for climate taxes)
- Automated corporate tax enforcement

**3. Post-Labor Economic Transition Planning**
- Sector-by-sector AI modeling of automation displacement timelines
- Reskilling pathway generators for at-risk workers
- Geographic concentration analysis of job automation risk
- Community economic transition planning (coal towns, manufacturing regions)
- New job creation forecasting

**4. AI as Economic Actor**
- AI agents that autonomously buy and sell goods and services
- Autonomous enterprises — companies with no human employees
- AI-driven microfinance and lending
- Automated negotiation agents (for housing, employment, services)
- AI investment managers (robo-advisors 2.0)

**5. Economic Identity & Value Creation**
- AI contribution tracking for value created by AI systems (GDP accounting reform?)
- Data as labor — AI models trained on user data generating value
- Personal AI agents as economic actors (your AI buys/sells on your behalf)
- Creative AI royalties and attribution systems

### Key Policies Being Debated

**Universal Basic Income (UBI)**
- Andrew Yang's Freedom Dividend ($1000/month) — US presidential campaign
- OpenLinux/Standing Rock Nations experiment
- Stockton SEED (Guaranteed Income) experiment
- Kenya UBI experiment (GiveDirectly)
- Alaska Permanent Fund (oil dividend — closest real-world model)

**Wealth Taxes**
- Elizabeth Warren's Ultra Millionaire Tax (2% on >$50M, 6% on >$1B)
- EU wealth tax proposals
- Property taxes, inheritance taxes, land value taxes

**Post-Labor Visions**
- Fully automated luxury communism — AI does all work, humans free for leisure
- Neo-feudalism dystopia — tiny elite, mass unemployment
- Stakeholder capitalism — companies optimizing for all stakeholders, not just shareholders
- Degrowth economics — intentionally shrinking economy for sustainability

### Companies & Research
- **GiveDirectly** — UBI experiments via cash transfers
- **OpenResearch (OpenAI founder)** — studying UBI effects
- **NBER** — economic research on automation and labor
- **IMF** — research on AI's impact on labor markets
- **World Economic Forum** — Future of Jobs report (updated annually)

### Ethical Considerations
1. **Deserve vs. need** — moral debates about who "deserves" support
2. **Work as identity** — what happens to human meaning if work disappears?
3. **Incentive effects** — does UBI reduce work motivation?
4. **Who pays** — how to tax AI-generated wealth without driving it elsewhere?
5. **Political economy** — who has power to implement these systems?

### Future Trajectory (2026-2030)
- First large-scale national UBI experiments (Finland already tested)
- "Automation adjustment benefit" — unemployment insurance for AI displacement
- Robot tax proposals advancing in EU and US legislatures
- Data dividend proposals — citizens get paid for data their AI generates
- National wealth funds (sovereign wealth funds for all citizens)
