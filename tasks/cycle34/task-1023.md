## Research: AI Agents in Climate Adaptation & Disaster Resilience Planning

### Overview
Even with aggressive emissions reduction, climate change is locked in for decades. AI agents are becoming essential for helping communities adapt — from predicting which coastal areas will flood to redesigning cities for extreme heat to managing the migration of climate refugees.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities in Climate Adaptation

**1. Climate Risk Assessment at Scale**
- AI that models which specific neighborhoods will flood, burn, or overheat
- Property-level climate risk scoring (used by insurers, real estate)
- Critical infrastructure vulnerability mapping
- Supply chain climate risk identification
- Multi-hazard scenario modeling (compound events: hurricane + flood + power outage)

**2. Urban Heat Island & Cooling Planning**
- AI that identifies urban heat islands from satellite imagery
- Tree canopy placement optimization for maximum cooling benefit
- Cool roof and cool pavement recommendation engine
- Heat warning system with targeted messaging
- Building-level heat exposure modeling

**3. Coastal Resilience & Managed Retreat**
- Sea level rise inundation modeling (NOAA + AI downscaling)
- AI that identifies properties for managed retreat (buyout prioritization)
- Living shoreline design recommendations
- Storm surge prediction and warning optimization
- Wetland migration corridor identification

**4. Disaster Response AI Agents**
- Real-time damage assessment from satellite imagery post-disaster
- Resource allocation optimization (where to send crews, supplies)
- Shelter capacity and placement optimization
- Evacuation route optimization under live traffic conditions
- Mutual aid network coordination

**5. Climate Migration & Relocation Planning**
- Community relocation planning for uninhabitable areas
- Cultural heritage preservation in relocation planning
- Housing market impact analysis of managed retreat
- Indigenous community relocation guidance
- Economic transition planning for fossil fuel communities

### Business Applications
- **The Climate Reality Project** — AI for climate adaptation planning
- **First Street Foundation** — property-level climate risk (flood, heat)
- **The Nature Conservancy** — AI for conservation and resilience planning
- **Moody's RMS** — catastrophe modeling for insurance
- **Google EIE (Environmental Insights Explorer)** — city-level carbon and climate data
- **Climate AI startups** — multiple companies applying AI to adaptation

### Ethical Considerations
1. **Managed retreat** — asking poor/marginalized communities to move, not wealthy ones
2. **Climate gentrification** — as cities adapt, who benefits from improvements?
3. **Intergenerational equity** — adaptation costs borne now for future generations
4. **Distributional justice** — who bears the cost of climate impacts vs. adaptation?
5. **Voluntary vs. forced relocation** — indigenous communities' rights to stay

### Challenges
1. **Long time horizons** — climate projections are for 2050/2100, hard to plan around
2. **Compound risks** — multiple hazards interacting in complex ways
3. **Data scarcity** — some regions have poor historical data for training
4. **Maladaptation** — well-intentioned adaptations that make things worse
5. **Insurance withdrawal** — as insurers exit high-risk areas, property values collapse

### Future Trajectory (2026-2030)
- Every coastal city has an AI-powered flood resilience system
- Climate adaptation as a standard part of urban planning
- AI that can predict climate refugees and help plan reception communities
- Real-time compound disaster response coordination
- "Adaptation passports" for properties (similar to energy ratings)
