## Research: AI Agents in Predictive Policing & Criminal Justice Reform

### Overview
Predictive policing and criminal justice AI are among the most ethically contested applications of AI. These systems promise to prevent crime and improve safety, but they also perpetuate historical biases, violate civil liberties, and raise fundamental questions about the proper role of technology in the carceral state.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities

**1. Predictive Policing Systems**
- Location-based crime prediction (Geolitica/PredPol — where to patrol)
- Person-based risk scoring (who is likely to commit or be a victim of crime)
- Real-time crime forecasting (hour-by-hour, district-by-district)
- ShotSpotter (acoustic gunshot detection with AI analysis)
- AI that generates patrol assignment recommendations

**2. Pre-Trial Risk Assessment**
- COMPAS (Northpointe) — recidivism risk scores used in bail/sentencing
- PSA (Arnold Foundation) — public safety assessment tool
- LSI-R (Level of Service Inventory) — criminogenic need assessment
- PAS (Pennsylvania) — pretrial risk assessment
- AI that recommends detention vs. release conditions

**3. Forensic AI**
- Facial recognition for suspect identification (controversial accuracy)
- Voice identification AI
- Fingerprint matching enhancement AI
- AI that analyzes gait, tattoos, and other biometric markers
- Forensic sketch to photo generation

**4. AI in Prosecution & Defense**
- Prosecutorial charging recommendation AI
- Sentencing guideline calculation AI
- Defense mitigation report generators
- AI that identifies wrongful conviction risk factors
- Predictive coding in e-discovery for litigation

**5. Prison & Reentry AI**
- Inmate classification and risk management AI
- Prison violence prediction (Solitary Watch)
- Reentry planning AI (housing, employment, treatment recommendations)
- Parole recommendation AI
- Phone call monitoring AI in prisons

### Landmark Cases & Evidence
- **People v. Burow (California)** — court suppressed AI-generated crime forecast as evidence
- **State v. Loomis (Wisconsin)** — COMPAS use upheld with conditions, required disclosure
- **FTC v. Kleemann** — algorithmic pricing algorithms in consumer markets
- **NIST FRVT** — ongoing facial recognition accuracy testing across demographics
- ProPublica "Machine Bias" (2016) — COMPAS showing significant racial disparities

### Racial Disparities in Criminal Justice AI
- Facial recognition: 10-100x worse accuracy for dark-skinned faces (NIST)
- COMPAS: similar recidivism scores for Black and white defendants, but false positives much higher for Black defendants
- Predictive policing: over-policing of Black neighborhoods perpetuates bias in training data
- LLM-generated legal documents: may systematically disadvantage non-English speakers

### Reform Movements
- **Accountability and Transparency in Pretrial Risk Assessment** — multiple states reforming or banning certain tools
- **Algorithmic Justice League** — advocacy for fair AI in criminal justice
- **Electronic Frontier Foundation** — surveillance reform
- **Partnership on AI** — responsible AI principles for justice applications

### Future Trajectory (2026-2030)
- Federal moratorium on facial recognition by law enforcement (some states already)
- Mandatory algorithmic impact assessments before deploying criminal justice AI
- Real-time bias auditing of risk assessment tools
- AI as defense counsel assistant (expanding access to justice)
- Shrinking role of predictive policing in favor of community-based approaches
