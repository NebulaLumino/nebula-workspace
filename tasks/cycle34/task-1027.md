## Research: AI Agents in Automated Administrative Law & Regulatory Adjudication

### Overview
Government agencies issue millions of administrative decisions every year — Social Security disability claims, immigration status determinations, tax assessments, environmental permits, food safety violations. AI is increasingly involved in these decisions, raising due process and administrative law questions that courts are only beginning to address.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities in Administrative Law

**1. Automated Government Adjudication**
- SSA disability claim determination AI (SSDI — massive scale)
- Unemployment benefits eligibility AI
- Immigration status processing AI (CBP One, visa processing)
- Tax assessment and audit selection AI
- SNAP (food stamps) eligibility AI
- Public housing eligibility AI

**2. AI in Regulatory Enforcement**
- OSHA violation pattern detection AI
- FDA food safety inspection AI (FSMA compliance)
- EPA environmental violation AI
- Financial regulatory compliance AI (SEC, FINRA)
- Healthcare fraud detection AI (CMS)
- Antitrust market analysis AI

**3. AI Administrative Law Judge Support**
- Decision recommendation engines for ALJs
- Precedent research and retrieval AI
- Evidence weighing support systems
- Brief generation for administrative proceedings
- Oral argument preparation AI
- Settlement valuation AI

**4. Rulemaking & Notice-and-Comment AI**
- AI that generates draft agency rules from policy directives
- Comment summarization AI (condensing thousands of public comments)
- Regulatory impact analysis AI (predicting economic effects of rules)
- Sunset clause recommendation AI
- Regulatory flexibility analysis

**5. FOIA and Government Transparency AI**
- Automated document declassification AI
- FOIA response generation AI
- Government contract transparency AI
- Spending data anomaly detection AI
- Public records request routing AI

### Administrative Law Principles at Stake
- **Due process** — Mathews v. Eldridge test for what process is due when government takes action
- **Arbitrary and capricious** — agency decisions must be reasoned and explained
- **Delegation doctrine** — Congress can't delegate too much power without standards
- **Chevron deference** — courts defer to agency interpretation of ambiguous statutes
- **Ex parte communications** — transparency in agency decision-making

### Key Legal Cases
- **Citizens to Preserve Overton Park v. Volpe (1971)** — highway funding and agency discretion
- **Motor Vehicle Manufacturers Assn. v. State Farm (1983)** — "arbitrary and capricious" standard for agency action
- **Wood v. Williams (N.D. Ill. 2023)** — challenge to SSA's ATMS AI for disability denials
- **HUD v. e品质 (2024)** — housing algorithm discrimination in Section 8

### Challenges
1. **Due process at scale** — cannot give individual hearings to millions of automated decisions
2. **Transparency** — how to explain a complex ML model to an affected individual?
3. **Legality of AI-only decisions** — some jurisdictions require human decision-makers
4. **Agency capture** — AI tools that make it easier to regulate may make enforcement less visible
