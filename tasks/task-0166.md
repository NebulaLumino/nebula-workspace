## Research Findings — AI Governance and Safety 2026

### Two Major Frameworks

**NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF)**
- Voluntary but increasingly required by US federal agencies, state laws, and the EU AI Act guidance
- Four core functions: Govern, Map, Measure, Manage
  - **Govern:** Establish culture of risk management, define accountability
  - **Map:** Identify and categorize AI risks in context
  - **Measure:** Analyze and track risks over time
  - **Manage:** Prioritize and act on risks
- NIST launched AI Agent Standards Initiative (2026) — agentic AI now treated as regulatory obligation
- Compatible with ISO 42001

**EU AI Act**
- Fully enforceable for high-risk systems: **August 2, 2026** ⚠️
- Risk-based classification:

| Risk Level | Examples | Requirements |
|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Unacceptable** | Social scoring, real-time biometric surveillance | 🚫 Prohibited |
| **High-risk** | Critical infrastructure, employment, law enforcement | ✅ Mandatory: risk management, data governance, human oversight, CE marking, EU registration |
| **Limited risk** | Chatbots, deepfakes | ⚠️ Transparency obligations (inform users) |
| **Minimal risk** | Most AI apps | ✅ No mandatory requirements |

- Penalties: Up to **€35M or 7% of global turnover**
- Applies to non-EU orgs whose AI impacts EU individuals

### AI Agent Governance (New Category)

Traditional AI governance = static model. Agent governance = dynamic autonomous system. New framework components:

- **Agent Identity & Scope:** What each agent is permitted to do; approved use cases
- **Human-in-the-Loop:** Not all decisions should be autonomous; checkpoints for critical actions
- **Access Controls:** Role-based data access for agents; sensitive data safeguards
- **Continuous Monitoring:** Real-time agent behavior tracking (not just static audits)
- **Explainability & Auditability:** Every agent decision traceable; critical for regulated industries
- **Agent Sprawl Problem:** Organizations deploying many agents without central oversight → need unified agent management platform

### Practical Compliance Steps (for deploying AI apps)

1. Conduct AI mapping exercise — what AI systems do you have?
2. Classify by risk level under EU AI Act
3. For high-risk: implement human oversight, logging, technical documentation
4. For limited risk (chatbots): add "AI-generated" disclosures
5. Monitor NIST AI Agent Standards Initiative for new requirements
6. Audit trails: log every significant agent decision (especially important for Nova deploying to others)
