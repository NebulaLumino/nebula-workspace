## Research Findings — AI Video Generation 2026

### The Big Players

| Tool | Provider | Best For | Key Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sora** | OpenAI | Photorealistic, long clips | Not publicly available yet |
| **Kling** | Kuaishou | 2-minute videos, physics | Character consistency in long clips |
| **Runway Gen-3 Alpha** | Runway | Cinematic quality, control | Short clips (10s), requires subscription |
| **Pika 2.0** | Pika Labs | Quick social clips | Motion quality |
| **Luma Dream Machine 3** | Luma AI | Image-to-video, consistency | Subject coherence in motion |
| **Hailuo AI** | MiniMax | Expressive motion, video-to-video | Limited output length |

### What Works in 2026
- **5-30 second clips** are generally high quality
- **Image-to-video** (upload image → animate it) works very well
- **Prompt adherence** is strong for style/atmosphere
- **Text-to-video** is improving but character consistency remains hard

### Key Limitations
- **Character/identity consistency**: The hardest problem — characters look different shot to shot
- **Physics**: Objects behave unrealistically (floating, clipping)
- **Text in video**: Almost impossible to render correctly
- **Duration**: Coherence drops significantly past 30 seconds

### Practical Use Cases (2026)

**Works well:**
- B-roll / stock footage alternatives (abstract backgrounds, atmospheric shots)
- Marketing/social content (logo animations, product showcases)
- Concept visualization (storyboarding, pitch decks)
- Image animation (old photo → living memory)

**Still impractical:**
- Character-driven narrative content
- Anything requiring readable text
- Videos over 1 minute without editing
