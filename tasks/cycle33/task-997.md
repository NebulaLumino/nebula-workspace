## Research: AI Agents in Digital Legacy & Post-Human Identity

### Overview
What happens to your digital presence after you die? As AI advances, the question of "digital legacy" is expanding from managing old social media accounts to something far more profound: can AI preserve, reconstruct, or continue aspects of a person's identity? This is emerging as a significant domain in AI ethics, law, and product design.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities in Digital Legacy

**1. Personal AI Historians & Memory Keepers**
- AI that learns your life story from journals, photos, letters, emails
- Conversational AI that family members can interact with after death ("Ask Grandpa Anything")
- Generational memory keepers (family history AI passed down)
- Legacy voice and personality preservation
- Products: StoryCorps (human recordings), HereAfter AI (life story recording), Replika (living memory)

**2. Grief Support AI**
- AI chatbots that simulate conversation with the deceased (controversial)
- Bereavement AI that helps mourners process grief
- AI that helps sort through the deceased's belongings and digital life
- Memory reconstruction from fragmented digital traces (texts, emails, photos)
- Legal AI that helps executors manage digital assets

**3. Digital Estate Planning AI**
- AI that helps organize and plan your digital estate (accounts, assets, passwords)
- Will clause generator for digital assets (crypto, accounts, creative works)
- AI fiduciary for digital asset management after death
- Automated account management post-mortem (memorialization, deletion)
- Digital inheritance transfer for crypto keys, NFTs, domain names

**4. Post-Human Identity Reconstruction**
- AI reconstruction of deceased person's communication style (from all their texts/emails)
- Voice cloning from audio recordings
- Photo and video generation of deceased (increasingly realistic)
- AI "versions" of deceased people in therapeutic or commemorative contexts
- Legally contested: deepfake of deceased celebrity for commercial use

**5. Consciousness Continuity Research**
- Brain simulation from detailed neural imaging (currently science fiction, but being researched)
- Connectomics: mapping the full neural connectome as basis for identity preservation
- Cryonics + future AI reconstruction (Nectome, Alcor)
- AI simulation of deceased person's decision-making patterns
- Emerging: "sufficiently advanced AI" that approximates the deceased's values

### Legal Landscape
- **GDPR** — right to be forgotten vs. right to digital legacy
- **EMIR** (EU) — executor rights to access digital assets
- **RUDAWS** (US state laws) — Revised Uniform Fiduciary Access to Digital Assets Act
- **California** — added digital assets to conservatorship law
- **No federal US law** yet covering digital estates comprehensively

### Products & Companies
- **HereAfter AI** — record your life story, family can chat with it
- **StoryCorps** — human-to-human recorded interviews (not AI)
- **Se影** — AI memorial portraits (controversial Chinese product)
- **Gensyn** — AI co-pilot for your personal knowledge
- **Proust AI** — AI trained on your digital footprint
- **1Password / LastPass** — digital estate planning for credentials
- **Google Inactive Account Manager** — Google's response to account death

### Ethical Considerations
1. **Consent** — did the deceased consent to being "brought back" as an AI?
2. **Mourning disruption** — does AI simulation of the deceased prevent healthy grief?
3. **Identity fraud** — deepfake deceased people for commercial or malicious purposes
4. **Family rights** — who controls the digital identity of a deceased family member?
5. **AI accuracy** — the AI simulation may not accurately represent the person
6. **Exploitation** — corporations monetizing the identities of dead people

### Future Trajectory (2026-2030)
- "Memorial AI" as a standard feature of social platforms (Google, Meta)
- AI that manages your digital presence proactively during your lifetime
- Legal recognition of "digital personhood" for estates
- Brain-computer interface companies (Neuralink, Synchron) potentially creating continuity between living and digital identity
- First AI simulation of a deceased person used in court (as character witness)
- Ethical guidelines from major AI labs on digital resurrection

### Questions to Explore
- Does everyone have the right to be forgotten, or the right to be remembered?
- Who should control a deceased person's AI-generated likeness — family, company, no one?
- At what point does an AI simulation of a deceased person deserve legal protection?
- Is it ethical for a young person to create an AI version of themselves pre-emptively?
- What happens to your AI legacy if the company that created it goes bankrupt?
