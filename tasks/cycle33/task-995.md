## Research: AI Agents in Atmospheric Science & Climate Intervention

### Overview
Climate change is the defining challenge of our era, and AI agents are becoming indispensable for understanding atmospheric systems, predicting extreme weather, and evaluating/managing climate intervention strategies. From solar geoengineering to carbon capture, AI is central to both understanding risks and designing solutions.

### Key AI Agent Capabilities in Atmospheric Science

**1. Ultra-High-Resolution Climate Modeling**
- AI downscaling global climate models to regional levels (km-scale resolution)
- Foundation models for Earth's climate (similar to LLMs for language)
- Surrogate models that approximate supercomputer climate simulations at 1000x speed
- Extreme event attribution: was this hurricane made more likely by climate change?
- Convection-permitting models for accurate rainfall prediction
- Models: GraphCast (Google DeepMind), Pangu-Weather (Huawei), NeuralGCM (Google)

**2. Solar Geoengineering AI**
- AI modeling of stratospheric aerosol injection (SAI) outcomes
- Optimal injection location and timing optimization
- Unintended consequence prediction (monsoon disruption, ozone effects)
- Monitoring and verification AI for geoengineering deployment
- Decision support for policy makers evaluating geoengineering

**3. Carbon Cycle & Sequestration AI**
- Carbon flux prediction from forests, oceans, soils
- Monitoring verification and carbon accounting (MRV) AI
- Identifying optimal locations for reforestation/afforestation
- Soil carbon sequestration optimization
- Direct air capture (DAC) facility optimization
- Blue carbon (ocean) monitoring and modeling

**4. Extreme Weather Prediction & Early Warning**
- Hurricane track and intensity forecasting (beyond current physics models)
- Tornado and severe storm prediction at lead times previously impossible
- Atmospheric river prediction for water resource management
- Wildfire risk modeling (fuel moisture, weather, terrain)
- Heat wave prediction and health impact modeling
- Flood prediction from precipitation forecasts
- Operational early warning systems for climate-vulnerable regions

**5. Climate Policy & Economic Modeling AI**
- Economic impact modeling of climate scenarios
- Just transition workforce planning (which communities need support?)
- Carbon pricing optimization AI
- Infrastructure resilience planning
- Insurance risk modeling for climate events
- Agricultural adaptation planning

### Ethical & Political Dimensions
- Solar geoengineering is deeply controversial and unilateral deployment could have global impacts
- "Climate intervention" vs. "climate mitigation" — are we giving up on emissions reduction?
- Historical responsibility for climate change affects who should pay for solutions
- Climate data colonialism — who owns climate data from the global south?

### Challenges
1. **Long time horizons** — climate models must validate over decades
2. **Tail risk** — rare extreme events are hardest to predict but most impactful
3. **Feedback loops** — climate has tipping points that are difficult to model
4. **Data scarcity** — some regions have few historical observations
5. **Political sensitivity** — climate science is politically fraught in some contexts

### Future Trajectory (2026-2030)
- AI climate models that run at kilometer-scale resolution globally
- First operational solar geoengineering monitoring system
- AI that can attribute any specific weather event to climate change in real-time
- Autonomous carbon monitoring from space (satellite + AI)
- Climate prediction that incorporates socioeconomic scenarios
- Geoengineering governance frameworks informed by AI modeling
