## Executive Summary

Finance was one of the earliest adopters of AI — and one of the most transformed. Quantitative trading has been algorithmic for decades, but AI is pushing this further: from rule-based systems to learning systems that discover patterns humans can't see. Beyond trading, AI is reshaping credit underwriting, fraud detection, insurance underwriting, and regulatory compliance. The financial sector's combination of rich data, high stakes, and regulatory scrutiny makes it a uniquely demanding environment for AI.

---

## Trading & Investment

### Quantitative Trading
- **Alpha generation**: AI finds subtle market patterns that generate returns
- **Statistical arbitrage**: Exploiting tiny price differences across exchanges/derivatives
- **Factor investing**: ML identifies which factors (value, momentum, quality) drive returns
- **High-frequency trading**: Sub-millisecond AI decisions on order routing and pricing

### Generative AI for Finance
- **Report generation**: Translating financial data into narratives (earnings summaries, analyst notes)
- **Research synthesis**: Reading thousands of SEC filings, transcripts, and news articles to inform investment decisions
- **Code generation**: AI writing and debugging quantitative trading strategies

### Notable Systems
- **Two Sigma, DE Shaw, Renaissance Technologies**: Pioneered ML in quant finance
- **JP Morgan's COIN**: Contract Intelligence — AI reviewing commercial loan agreements; saved 360,000 hours of human work annually
- **Kensho (S&P Global)**: AI for financial analytics and data synthesis

## Credit & Underwriting

### AI Underwriting
- **Alternative data**: Using non-traditional signals (rent payments, subscription data, social behavior) for credit scoring
- **Traditional FICO alternatives**: Companies like Experian, Equifax building ML models; startups like Zego, Lenddo
- **Fair lending compliance**: AI models must be explainable for regulatory compliance (ECOA, Fair Housing Act)

### Embedded Finance
- **Buy-now-pay-later**: AI-driven instant credit decisions at point of sale
- **SMB lending**: AI analyzing cash flow patterns, POS data, and e-commerce metrics for small business loans

## Fraud Detection

### Real-Time Transaction Monitoring
- **Anomaly detection**: AI flags unusual spending patterns in milliseconds
- **Behavioral biometrics**: Typing speed, touch pressure, mouse movement as fraud signals
- **Graph neural networks**: Detecting fraud rings by mapping relationships between entities

### Account Takeover
- **Login behavior analysis**: AI detecting when an account is accessed by an unauthorized user
- **Voice ID**: Biometric voice authentication for phone banking

### Key Players
- **Feedzai**: AI fraud platform for banks and merchants
- **Signifyd**: E-commerce fraud protection
- **Forter**: Real-time fraud decisions for digital commerce

## Risk Management

### Market Risk
- **VaR models**: ML improving Value-at-Risk calculations
- **Stress testing**: AI generating realistic adverse scenarios for regulatory stress tests
- **Systemic risk detection**: Identifying interconnectedness that could trigger contagion

### Operational Risk
- **Model risk management**: AI monitoring AI models for drift, bias, and failure
- **Regulatory reporting**: AI automating Basel, DORA, and other compliance reporting

### Insurance
- **Claims automation**: AI handling firstNotice of loss, fraud detection, and claims triage
- **Parametric insurance**: AI-triggered payouts based on objective data (earthquake sensors, weather data)

---

## Regulatory Landscape

### Key Regulations
- **SR 11-7 (Fed/OCC)**: Model risk management guidance for financial institutions
- **ECAI (EU)**: AI credit scoring systems must be transparent and non-discriminatory
- **DORA (EU, 2025)**: Digital Operational Resilience Act — AI systems must be tested for resilience
- **FDIA**: FDA-style approval process for AI credit models in consumer lending (proposed)

### Explainability Requirements
- **Adverse action notices**: When AI denies credit, lenders must explain why (ECOA)
- **GDPR right to explanation**: EU requirement for algorithmic decisions to be explainable
- **Trading transparency**: MiFID II requires algorithmic trading systems to be auditable

---

## Risks

### Flash Crashes
- AI trading algorithms have caused multiple flash crashes (2010, 2015, 2022)
- Feedback loops and herding behavior are harder to predict with AI than humans

### Model Overfitting
- Backtesting shows great results; live trading shows losses — classic quant problem
- Market regime changes make historically-trained models unreliable

### Systemic Risk
- When multiple AI systems react to the same signals simultaneously, they can amplify market moves
- 2022 gilt market crisis partly attributed to hedgers all selling simultaneously

---

## Bottom Line

Finance will be one of the most deeply AI-transformed industries because the economic incentives are enormous and the data is rich. The firms that win will have three things: superior data, superior AI models, and rigorous model risk management. The ones that lose will face regulatory backlash, model failures, or algorithmic flash crashes.
