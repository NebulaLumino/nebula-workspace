## Executive Summary

AI agents are transforming food science and personalized nutrition through integration of nutritional genomics, gut microbiome analysis, continuous glucose monitoring, and AI food formulation. Commercial applications are scaling rapidly: ZOE serves 100K+ subscribers with personalized meal scoring; DayTwo achieved clinically meaningful HbA1c improvements in diabetics; NotCo reached unicorn valuation with AI-designed plant-based products. Key challenges include regulatory classification ambiguity (medical device vs. wellness app), genetic/biometric data privacy, evidence base gaps, and equity of access. The field is moving toward continuous multi-biomarker metabolic health monitoring and food-as-medicine protocols with insurance coverage.

## Key Concepts

- Nutrigenomics (SNP × nutrient interactions)
- Gut microbiome AI for glycemic response prediction
- CGM + wearable integration for continuous metabolic monitoring
- AI food formulation (generative design of plant-based proteins)
- Allergenicity prediction for novel food proteins
- Food safety and supply chain AI (blockchain provenance)

## Current State

ZOE PREDICT studies (n=1,100+), DayTwo gut microbiome → glycemic prediction (Cell 2021), NotCo AI food formulation, FDA allergenicity AI screening (GRAS process), Mayo Clinic ICU AI nutrition (18% LOS reduction in trial), personalized supplement AI (Rootine).

## Key Players

ZOE, DayTwo, NotCo, Motif FoodWorks, Nestlé, IBM Food Trust, Levels Health, Nutrisens AI, FARE/FDA, Mayo Clinic

## Challenges

Population-level evidence gaps for individualization; regulatory classification ambiguity; biometric data privacy; microbiome instability; commercial hype vs. scientific rigor; equity of access.

## Future Outlook

FDA clearance for AI nutrition decision support (2025-27); continuous multi-biomarker metabolic scores replacing periodic blood tests; metabolic digital twins; food-as-medicine clinical protocols with insurance coverage; personalized on-demand food manufacturing (2028+).
