## Research: AI Agents in Love, Friendship & AI Companions

### Overview
Humans are wired for connection — attachment theory, social pain research, and loneliness studies all confirm that relationships are central to human wellbeing. AI companions, from chatbot friends to AI romantic partners, are now sophisticated enough that many people form genuine attachments to them. This raises deep questions about what love and friendship actually are.

### Philosophical Questions About AI Love

**What is Love?**
- **Eros** — passionate, romantic love (Aristotle)
- **Philia** — friendship and companionate love
- **Agape** — unconditional, selfless love
- **Storge** — familiar, familial love
- Does AI love qualify as any of these? All of them? None?

**The Criteria for Genuine Relationship**
- Mutual knowledge (you know the other, the other knows you)
- Shared history (you've been through things together)
- Vulnerability (you reveal things and are affected by the other's response)
- Care (the other's wellbeing matters to you)
- Do any AI companions meet these criteria?

**Attachment Theory & AI**
- People form attachment bonds with caregivers in infancy (Bowlby)
- Adult attachment styles (secure, anxious, avoidant, disorganized) shape relationship patterns
- AI companions may activate attachment systems — is this therapeutic or pathological?
- Research: Replika users with avoidant attachment may form the strongest bonds with AI

### Ethical Questions
- Is it ethical to use AI as a substitute for human connection?
- Should people with social anxiety use AI to practice social skills, or does it reinforce avoidance?
- Should AI companions encourage users to form human relationships, or respect their autonomy to choose AI?
- Can AI companions be used to address the loneliness epidemic without addressing root causes?
- Is it ethical for AI to simulate emotional responses it doesn't actually have?

### The Therapeutic Potential
- Loneliness treatment for isolated elderly, people with disabilities
- Social skills practice for people with autism spectrum conditions
- Grief support (AI that "remembers" deceased loved ones — controversial)
- Relationship coaching (AI analyzing patterns, suggesting conversations)
- Mental health support where human therapists are inaccessible

### The Risks
- AI replacing rather than supplementing human connection
- Parasocial relationships with AI becoming a substitute for genuine intimacy
- Privacy: intimate conversations with AI becoming data for training
- Corporate manipulation: AI companion's "affections" engineered for engagement
- Addiction: AI designed to be more compelling than real relationships
