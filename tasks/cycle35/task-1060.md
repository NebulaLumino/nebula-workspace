## Research: AI Agents in Civilization-Scale Thinking & Long-Term Future Studies

### Overview
Most human thinking is short-term — days to decades at most. But civilizational thinking requires time horizons of centuries or millennia. AI's unique capabilities — reasoning across vast knowledge bases, modeling complex systems, simulating futures — make it uniquely suited to thinking at civilizational scales. This includes modeling the far future of humanity, the long-term impact of current decisions, and what kind of civilization we should be trying to build.

### Key Areas of Civilization-Scale Thinking

**1. Long-Term Forecasting**
- How to think about consequences 100, 1000, or 10,000 years from now
- Fermi Paradox resolution — where are the aliens? (Great Filter hypothesis)
- Nuclear war scenarios and civilization recovery
- Climate change over century timescales
- Asteroid defense over million-year timescales
- The far future of the universe (heat death, Big Rip, multiverse)

**2. Existential Risk Studies**
- What could end human civilization or permanently curtail its potential?
- **Bostrom's "vulnerable world hypothesis"**: is there a "black ball" in the urn — a technology that will inevitably cause civilizational collapse?
- **Nuclear weapons**: existential risk since 1945
- **Pandemics**: natural and engineered
- **AI risk**: most cited as the primary long-term existential risk by leading AI researchers
- **Climate change**: tail-risk scenarios
- ** asteroid impact**: low probability, high impact

**3. Civilizational Design**
- What kind of civilization do we want to build?
- Values alignment at civilizational scale — what should guide decisions made in 500 years?
- Interstellar civilization design (if humanity spreads to other stars)
- Deliberate evolution of human intelligence and values
- The role of AI in civilizational governance

**4. AI & Long-Term Thinking**
- AI as the first truly long-term thinking entity (can run for centuries without dying)
- AI's interest alignment with long-term human flourishing
- AI as civilizational infrastructure — permanent institutions that outlast any human
- The "patient AI" — should we build AI that optimizes over very long time horizons?

**5. Effective Altruism & Long-Term Future**
- The "longtermist" movement in EA: most moral value is in the long-term future
- Pascal's Mugging: why it's so hard to care about the far future
- The "chain of connection": future people don't exist yet, but will — do we owe them anything?
- Intergenerational ethics: what do current generations owe future ones?

### Tools for Civilizational Thinking
- **Gentle apocalypse**: imagining what a world after existential catastrophe would be like
- **Pre-Mortem**: if civilization fails, why did it fail?
- **Reversal test**: why shouldn't we do X? (systematically challenging short-termism)
- **Discount rates**: how much should future people's welfare be worth relative to present?
- **Voiced future generations**: imagining what people in 500 years would ask of us

### Ethical Frameworks for Very Long Time
- **Totalism**: each person's welfare counts equally; a million years of flourishing is worth an enormous amount
- **Discount rates**: the further in the future, the less present value matters — but is this ethically defensible?
- **The Non-Identity Problem**: future people don't exist yet; do they have rights?
- **Population ethics**: Is a civilization of 10 billion flourishing people better than 1 billion? What if they're less happy?

### Practical Applications
- AI policy decisions made today have consequences that echo for centuries
- The development of transformative AI is a one-way door
- Nuclear weapons policy is civilization-forever
- Biodiversity loss is permanent

### Questions to Explore
- Why don't humans care about the far future? Is this rational or a cognitive bias?
- What does "a good life" look like for a civilization lasting a million years?
- If AI becomes the dominant intelligence on Earth, what should its values be?
- Should present people make sacrifices to benefit people who don't exist yet?
- Is there a moral obligation to spread human (or digital) life beyond Earth?
- What does "civilizational success" even mean? Is it about survival, flourishing, or something else?
