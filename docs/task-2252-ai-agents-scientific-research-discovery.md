# Task 2252: Explore AI Agents in Scientific Research, Drug Discovery & Academia AI

## Overview
AI is accelerating scientific discovery at unprecedented speed — from AlphaFold predicting protein structures to AI-driven drug discovery pipelines compressing years of research into months. The implications for human health and scientific knowledge are profound.

## Key Applications
- **Protein structure prediction**: AlphaFold (DeepMind) predicts 3D protein structures from amino acid sequences — Nobel Prize-level impact
- **Drug discovery**: AI screens millions of compounds for drug-target interactions, dramatically accelerating early-stage drug discovery
- **Materials science**: AI discovers new battery materials, superconductors, and catalysts
- **Literature synthesis**: AI reads and synthesizes millions of papers to generate research hypotheses
- **Lab automation**: AI controls robotic lab equipment to run high-throughput experiments
- **Peer review assistance**: AI flags papers for ethical issues, statistical errors, or suitability

## Tools & Frameworks
- **AlphaFold (DeepMind)**: Protein structure prediction (DB: 200M+ structures)
- **RoseTTAFold (Baker Lab)**: Alternative protein structure prediction
- **IBM RxNN / Insilico Medicine**: AI drug discovery pipelines
- **DeepChem**: Open-source AI for chemistry and drug discovery
- **Elicit / Consensus**: AI research paper search and synthesis
- **SciSpace (Typeset)**: AI paper understanding and explanation

## Ethical Considerations
- **Access inequality**: AI scientific tools are concentrated in wealthy institutions and Big Pharma
- **Hype vs. reality**: Many AI drug discovery claims don't survive clinical trials
- **Research integrity**: AI-assisted writing may mask poor research design
- **Dual use**: AI that accelerates drug discovery also accelerates bioweapon research
- **Scientific workforce**: AI will eliminate many research technician and postdoc positions

## Key Players
DeepMind, Baker Lab (University of Washington), Insilico Medicine, Relay Therapeutics, Exscientia, DeepChem
