# AI Agents in Quantum Computing Applications

## What AI Agents Do in This Domain

AI agents in quantum computing serve two critical roles: optimizing quantum circuit design (where classical AI helps quantum systems work better) and solving classically-intractable problems that arise in real-world quantum applications. The relationship between AI and quantum computing is symbiotic — AI helps quantum, and quantum will help AI.

## Key Capabilities

**Quantum Circuit Optimization**
AI agents like those from Google's Quantum AI team use reinforcement learning to discover more efficient quantum circuits — reducing gate depth and error rates faster than human-designed approaches. AlphaQubit (DeepMind) applies machine learning to improve quantum error correction.

**Hybrid Classical-Quantum Optimization**
For logistics, finance, and drug discovery, AI agents orchestrate hybrid workflows that offload specific subproblems to quantum annealers (D-Wave) or gate-model quantum computers while handling the rest classically. Companies like BBVA and Goldman Sachs use these approaches for portfolio optimization.

**Quantum Machine Learning**
Quantum computers can process vast feature spaces for ML models. AI agents help determine which ML problems are genuinely suited to quantum speedup vs. classical efficiency, directing computational resources appropriately.

**Quantum Chemistry Simulation**
AI agents drive quantum simulations of molecular behavior — enabling drug discovery and materials science by simulating how molecules interact at quantum mechanical level. Roche and Merck use quantum + AI approaches for pharmaceutical R&D.

## Challenges
- Quantum hardware is still noisy and limited (NISQ era)
- Most "quantum advantage" claims are theoretical or narrow
- Hybrid workflows require significant classical ML expertise
- Quantum computing talent is extremely scarce

## Current State
Most real-world quantum AI applications remain in research labs and pilot programs. Practical commercial applications are limited to optimization problems in finance and logistics. True AI agents with autonomous quantum task routing are nascent — the field is moving toward AI systems that "know when to use quantum" for each subproblem.
