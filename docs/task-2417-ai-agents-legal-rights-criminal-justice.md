# Task 2417: Explore AI Agents in Criminal Justice, Bail & Sentencing Reform AI

## Overview
The US criminal justice system uses AI at nearly every stage — from predictive policing and bail decisions to sentencing recommendations and risk assessment. These systems have enormous power over people's lives and have been shown to encode racial and socioeconomic bias.

## Key Applications
- **Risk assessment scores**: AI predicts recidivism risk for bail and sentencing (COMPAS, LSI-R)
- **Facial recognition for mugshots**: AI matches suspects to databases
- **Predictive policing**: AI directs police patrol to high-crime areas
- **Evidence assessment**: AI helps prosecutors evaluate strength of evidence
- **Sentencing recommendations**: AI generates sentencing guideline recommendations
- **Jail healthcare triage**: AI determines medical care priority in prisons

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Equivant (COMPAS)**: Risk assessment AI used in sentencing
- **Northpointe (Correctional Offender Management)**: Risk and needs assessment
- **Clearview AI**: Facial recognition for law enforcement
- **PredPol (now Geolitica)**: Predictive policing AI
- **Knight Analytics**: Sentencing recommendation tools

## Ethical Considerations
- **Racial bias**: COMPAS and similar tools have been shown to falsely flag Black defendants as higher risk
- **Due process**: Defendants rarely know AI was used or how it reached its conclusions
- **No accountability**: No one is responsible when an AI recommendation is wrong
- **Self-fulfilling prophecy**: AI risk scores that recommend jail increase recidivism by exposing defendants to incarceration
- **Evidence paradox**: AI that appears objective can encode historical discrimination into seemingly neutral numbers

## Key Players
Equivant, Northpointe, Clearview AI, civil liberties organizations (ACLU litigation), reform advocates
