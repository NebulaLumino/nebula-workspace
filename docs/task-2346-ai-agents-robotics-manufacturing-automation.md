# Task 2346: Explore AI Agents in Robotics, Manufacturing Automation & Cobots AI

## Overview
Robotics + AI is one of the most tangible AI applications — physical machines doing physical work with AI guidance. From Tesla's humanoid robots to warehouse automation to surgical robots, AI-powered robots are entering every industry.

## Key Applications
- **Warehouse logistics**: Amazon robots, Sortly, and others handle millions of packages
- **Manufacturing assembly**: AI-guided robotic arms perform increasingly complex assembly tasks
- **Surgical robotics**: Da Vinci and other systems assist or perform surgeries
- **Autonomous vehicles**: Self-driving cars, trucks, and delivery robots
- **Agriculture robots**: Robotic harvesters, weeders, and milkers
- **Cobots (collaborative robots)**: Robots designed to work alongside humans safely
- **Humanoid robots**: Tesla Optimus, Figure, and others attempt general-purpose physical tasks

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Tesla Optimus**: AI humanoid robot project
- **Figure AI**: General-purpose humanoid robot
- **Boston Dynamics (Hyundai)**: Advanced mobile robots (Atlas, Spot)
- **ABB Robotics**: Industrial AI robotics
- **iRobot (Amazon)**: Consumer and warehouse robots
- **Da Vinci Surgical (Intuitive Surgical)**: Surgical robotics leader
- **Covariant (AI robotics)**: General-purpose picking robots

## Ethical Considerations
- **Manufacturing job displacement**: The largest wave of automation displacement in history
- **Safety and liability**: When a robot injures someone, who is liable — the manufacturer, the programmer, or the deployer?
- **Cobot safety**: Can humans and robots safely share workspaces at scale?
- **Weaponization**: Military robots and armed drones are a direct threat to human life
- **Surveillance via robots**: Warehouse and domestic robots collect enormous amounts of environmental data
