# Task 2195: Explore AI Agents in Sports Analytics & Wearable Device AI

## Overview
Wearables (smartwatches, GPS trackers, smart mouthguards, heart rate monitors) have generated unprecedented volumes of athlete biometric data. AI is the tool that turns this firehose of data into actionable insights.

## Key Applications
- **Load monitoring**: AI calculates acute:chronic workload ratios to prevent overtraining and injury
- **Sleep optimization**: AI analyzes sleep data to optimize training scheduling around recovery
- **HRV analysis**: AI detects early signs of illness or overreaching from heart rate variability
- **GPS + accelerometer**: AI tracks position, speed, acceleration, and collision events in team sports
- **Metabolic modeling**: AI estimates energy expenditure from HR + accelerometer data
- **Smart mouthguards**: AI detects concussion risk from head impact data

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Whoop**: AI-driven strain and recovery platform
- **Catapult GPS**: Team sport wearable tracking
- **Oura Ring**: Sleep and recovery AI
- **Whoop / Whoop API**: 3rd party integration for AI analytics
- **Zebra (Sports)**: Real-time location tracking in stadiums
- **Fitbits / Garmin**: Consumer wearable AI

## Ethical Considerations
- **Privacy of biometric data**: Who owns athlete biometric data — athlete, team, league, or wearable company?
- **Surveillance vs. optimization**: Data-driven monitoring can become surveillance that undermines athlete autonomy
- **Fairness across sports**: Wealthy teams with better wearables have unfair performance advantages
- **Mental health data**: HRV and sleep data can reveal mental health struggles — how is this protected?
