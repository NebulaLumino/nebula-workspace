# Task 2315: Explore AI Agents in Mental Health Biometrics, Voice Analysis & Emotion Recognition AI

## Overview
Mental health diagnosis has always relied on subjective self-report. AI biometric analysis — voice patterns, speech cadence, facial micro-expressions, and keystroke dynamics — offers the possibility of objective, continuous mental health monitoring. The ethical stakes are enormous.

## Key Applications
- **Voice-based depression detection**: AI analyzes speech patterns (pitch, pause length, word choice) to detect depression severity
- **PTSD detection**: Voice stress analysis for trauma detection (used by the VA)
- **Suicide risk prediction**: AI detects linguistic markers in clinical notes and speech that predict suicide attempts
- **Emotion recognition**: AI reads facial expressions and voice to classify emotional states (controversial)
- **Schizophrenia early detection**: AI analyzes speech coherence and semantic patterns to detect early schizophrenia
- **ADHD assessment**: AI analyzes activity patterns and response times for ADHD diagnosis support
- **Kides**: AI mental health monitoring for kids via smart device patterns

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Ellipsis Health**: AI voice analysis for mental health assessment
- **Kintsugi (voice AI)**: Depression and anxiety detection from voice
- **X2 AI (Woebot)**: Conversational AI mental health monitoring
- **Cogito (healthcare AI)**: Voice analytics for call center mental health screening
- **Audio2**: Mental health biomarkers from voice
- **NIH voice analysis research**: Academic research on voice as mental health biomarker

## Ethical Considerations
- **Accuracy and bias**: Voice AI performs differently across accented English, dialects, and non-native speakers
- **Surveillance vs. care**: Continuous monitoring can feel supportive or surveillance-like
- **False positives**: AI detecting "mental illness" in healthy people causes harm
- **Consent complexity**: When insurers or employers deploy this, consent is coerced
- **Emotion recognition controversy**: Psychological research questions whether emotions can be reliably classified from faces/voice

Ellipsis Health, Kintsugi, Cogito, X2 AI, NeuroLex, NIH
