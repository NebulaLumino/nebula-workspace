# Task 2350: Explore AI Agents in Exoplanet Discovery, Astrobiology & Space Science AI

## Overview
The search for extraterrestrial life — and the cataloging of thousands of exoplanets — is one of the most data-intensive scientific endeavors. AI is essential to processing telescope data, identifying biosignatures, and planning missions to search for life.

## Key Applications
- **Exoplanet detection**: AI analyzes TESS and Kepler telescope data to identify planet candidates
- **Biosignature analysis**: AI searches atmospheric spectrograph data for signs of life
- **SETI signal processing**: AI scans radio telescope data for artificial signals
- **Spacecraft navigation**: AI guides autonomous spacecraft and rovers
- **Mission planning**: AI optimizes interplanetary mission trajectories and science operations
- **Mars rovers**: AI assists with autonomous navigation and science target selection

## Tools & Frameworks
- **NASA Exoplanet Archive (AI tools)**: Planet candidate identification
- **Google DeepMind + ESA**: AI for analyzing telescope data
- **SETI Institute AI initiatives**: Signal processing for alien detection
- **NASA Perseverance (Mastcam-Z)**: AI-assisted Martian geology
- **JPL Mission Planning AI**: Interplanetary mission trajectory optimization
- **Berkeley SETI Research Center**: Breakthrough Listen AI analysis

## Ethical Considerations
- **The "great filter"**: If we find abundant life-friendly exoplanets, why do we see no signs of civilizations? The Fermi Paradox has dark implications
- **Contamination risk**: AI-driven missions to potentially habitable worlds must avoid contaminating them with Earth life
- **First contact protocols**: If AI detects an alien signal, who decides? There's no global protocol
- **SETI resource inequality**: Only wealthy nations can participate in SETI — global benefit-sharing questions
- **Philosophical framework**: What AI philosophical assumptions shape how we interpret potential biosignatures?

## Key Players
NASA JPL, ESA, SETI Institute, DeepMind (astrophysics research), Berkeley SETI, Breakthrough Listen initiative
