# Task 2230: Explore AI Agents in Digital Fashion, NFT Clothing & Virtual Wardrobe AI

## Overview
Digital fashion — clothing that exists only in virtual form for avatars, games, and AR — is a rapidly growing market. AI is at the center: generating digital garments, powering virtual try-on, and creating the infrastructure for a new kind of fashion economy.

## Key Applications
- **AI garment generation**: Generate 3D garment designs from text or sketch for virtual worlds
- **Digital twin wardrobe**: AI creates a virtual version of your real wardrobe for AR/virtual try-on
- **Avatar clothing**: AI generates clothing for gaming avatars and metaverse platforms
- **NFT fashion design**: Design and mint fashion NFTs as collectible or wearable digital items
- **AR clothing try-on**: AI overlays virtual clothing on real bodies via smartphone AR
- **Virtual fashion for metaverse**: AI creates collections for Decentraland, Roblox, and Fortnite skins
- **Sustainable fashion alternative**: Virtual fashion as a zero-waste alternative to physical clothing purchases

## Tools & Frameworks
- **The Fabricant**: Digital fashion house using AI for virtual garment creation
- **DressX**: Digital fashion marketplace for Instagram AR and metaverse
- **Carlings**: "Last Statement" — digital-only clothing collection
- **RTFKT (Nike)**: Virtual sneakers and NFT fashion
- **MetaHuman (Epic Games)**: Photorealistic avatar technology for fashion try-on
- **Zero10**: AR virtual try-on for retail

## Ethical Considerations
- **Environmental cost of blockchain**: NFT fashion has significant energy footprint despite "sustainable" marketing
- **Digital exclusion**: Only people with VR/AR equipment can participate
- **Speculative bubbles**: NFT fashion value is highly speculative and has crashed dramatically
- **Labor in virtual fashion**: Digital garment workers face their own labor issues
- **Body image in virtual fashion**: Virtual fashion could reduce physical self-esteem while appearing to be a "body positive" alternative
