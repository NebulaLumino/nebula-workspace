# Task 2347: Explore AI Agents in Hiring, Recruitment Bias & Employment Law AI

## Overview
AI hiring tools promise to remove human bias — but evidence shows they often encode and amplify existing biases. The field sits at the intersection of civil rights law, labor economics, and technology, with regulators beginning to act.

## Key Applications
- **Resume screening**: AI filters resumes before human review, optimizing for keywords and patterns
- **Video interview analysis**: AI scores candidates on facial expressions, tone, and word choice (controversial)
- **Assessment games**: AI-driven cognitive and personality games to evaluate candidates
- **Salary prediction**: AI suggests starting salaries that may encode historical pay inequities
- **Internal mobility**: AI matches current employees to open roles based on skills
- **Onboarding AI**: AI guides new hires through orientation and training

## Tools & Frameworks
- **HireVue (video AI)**: AI-assisted video interviews — faced backlash and discontinued visual analysis
- **Pymetrics (neuroscience games)**: Gamified assessments
- **Eightfold.ai (talent intelligence)**: Skills ontology and matching
- **Greenhouse (hiring platform AI)**: Candidate sourcing and scoring
- **Lattice (performance AI)**: Performance management and promotion prediction

## Ethical Considerations
- **EEOC compliance**: US law prohibits hiring practices that disproportionately harm protected classes — AI hiring tools can violate this
- **Bias audits**: Most AI hiring tools have never been independently audited for bias
- **Adverse impact**: AI may filter out candidates from underrepresented groups even without explicit intent
- **Transparency**: Candidates often don't know they're being evaluated by AI
- **Skills vs. credentials**: AI may systematically undervalue non-traditional backgrounds

HireVue, Pymetrics, Eightfold.ai, Greenhouse, Lattice, Beamery, Paradox (Olivia AI recruiter)
