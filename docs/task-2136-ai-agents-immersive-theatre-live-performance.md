# Task 2136: Explore AI Agents in Immersive Theatre & Live Performance AI

## Overview
Immersive theatre (think Punchdrunk's "Sleep No More") and live performance are pushing the boundaries of AI — not just for production but for real-time audience interaction, generative set design, and adaptive storytelling.

## Key Applications

### Real-Time Audience Interaction
- **Facial/voice recognition for personalization**: AI systems that respond to audience member emotions or choices
- **Chatbot NPCs**: AI-driven characters that hold unscripted conversations with audience members
- **Adaptive narratives**: AI adjusts the story based on audience collective choices in real-time

### Production Design
- **Generative set design**: AI generates environment concepts for theatrical spaces
- **Projection mapping + AI**: Dynamic AI-driven projections that respond to performer movement
- **Costume concept generation**: AI mood boards for costume designers

### Performative AI
- **AI as performer**: Robots and AI-driven screens as co-performers in live theatre
- **Voice synthesis for character**: Realistic synthetic voices for non-speaking roles or historical figures
- **AI-generated music/sound**: Adaptive soundscapes that respond to live performance

## Tools & Frameworks

### Commercial
- ** Notch **: Real-time generative visual for live events
- **Millumin**: AI-driven show control for immersive experiences
- ** Cycling '74 Max/MSP + AI**: Audio-visual live coding
- **Hide & Seek (The VOID)**: VR immersive theatre

### Open Source / Research
- **openFrameworks + TensorFlow**: Custom AI performance systems
- **GPT-4 + Whisper real-time**: Conversational AI NPCs
- **Midjourney / Stable Diffusion**: Set and costume concept art

## Ethical Considerations
- **Consent and surveillance**: Facial recognition in immersive experiences raises privacy concerns for audiences who may not expect to be tracked
- **Displacement of performers**: AI-driven "performers" may replace human actors in some contexts
- **Audience manipulation**: Real-time psychological profiling of audience members for "better experiences" raises ethical red flags
- **Artist labor**: AI tools can reduce the labor needed to create immersive experiences, potentially reducing employment

## Future Outlook
AI-driven immersive theatre is moving from novelty to standard practice. Within 5 years, audience members may have genuinely unique narrative experiences each time they attend a show — with AI tailoring the story to their specific emotional and narrative preferences in real time.
