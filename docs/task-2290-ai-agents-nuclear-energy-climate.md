# Task 2290: Explore AI Agents in Nuclear Energy, Climate Modeling & Environmental Science AI

## Overview
Climate change is the defining challenge of our time. AI is being deployed across climate science — from improving climate models to optimizing nuclear reactors to accelerating the clean energy transition. The intersection of AI's enormous computational demands and its potential to reduce emissions is a complex story.

## Key Applications
- **Climate modeling**: AI improves the resolution and accuracy of climate models, reducing uncertainty in predictions
- **Fusion reactor control**: AI controls plasma in tokamak fusion reactors (DeepMind + EPFL achieved groundbreaking plasma control)
- **Nuclear plant monitoring**: AI monitors for anomalies, predicts maintenance needs, and improves safety in existing reactors
- **Carbon capture optimization**: AI optimizes the chemistry of direct air capture and carbon sequestration
- **Clean energy grid management**: AI balances renewable intermittency and optimizes energy storage
- **Emissions monitoring**: AI processes satellite imagery to detect methane leaks and deforestation
- **Extreme weather prediction**: AI improves hurricane, wildfire, and flood prediction models

## Tools & Frameworks
- **DeepMind + EPFL (TCV tokamak)**: AI plasma control for fusion
- **Climate AI startups**: Climate.ai, Climate Engine, Descartes Labs
- **NVIDIA Earth-2**: Supercomputer for climate simulation
- **Carbon capture AI**: Cquest, CarbonCure (concrete AI)
- **IRENA (renewables AI)**: International Renewable Energy Agency AI initiatives
- **Google Environmental Insights Explorer**: Building emissions data AI

## Ethical Considerations
- **AI's own carbon footprint**: Training large AI models has significant carbon footprint — contradictory to climate goals
- **Nuclear safety**: AI in nuclear applications requires extremely high reliability and failsafe design
- **Climate data colonialism**: AI climate models trained primarily on Western data may miss Global South dynamics
- **Dual-use**: Climate modeling tools can also improve weather forecasting for military applications
- **Nuclear proliferation**: AI-accelerated nuclear fuel cycle research could lower proliferation barriers

## Key Players
DeepMind, EPFL, NVIDIA (Earth-2), CarbonCure, Climate.ai, Descartes Labs, IRENA, NOAA AI initiatives
