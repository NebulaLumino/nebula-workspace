# AI Agents in Nuclear Energy & Climate

## Introduction

The intersection of artificial intelligence and nuclear energy represents one of the most promising frontiers in the fight against climate change. AI agents are now being deployed across the nuclear lifecycle — from reactor safety monitoring and anomaly detection to fusion research and small modular reactor (SMR) design optimization. This convergence is accelerating progress toward cleaner, safer, and more efficient nuclear power.

## AI in Nuclear Reactor Safety Monitoring

Nuclear reactors generate enormous amounts of sensor data in real time. AI agents excel at processing this data to detect patterns that human operators might miss. Machine learning models trained on historical reactor operation data can identify early warning signs of equipment degradation, coolant anomalies, or neutron flux irregularities before they become critical.

Companies like **Exxa** (formerly GE Hitachi Nuclear Energy) are using AI-powered digital twins to simulate reactor behavior, enabling predictive maintenance and reducing unplanned downtime. These digital twin systems create a continuous virtual replica of the physical reactor, flagging deviations that warrant human review.

## AI in Fusion Energy Research

Perhaps the most dramatic AI breakthrough in nuclear science has come from fusion energy. In 2022, DeepMind and EPFL published results from a deep reinforcement learning system that learned to control plasma stability in a tokamak fusion reactor — a task so complex that it had previously required teams of human experts tuning thousands of parameters. The AI discovered novel control strategies that outperformed human-designed approaches.

**Commonwealth Fusion Systems** and **TAE Technologies** are using AI to optimize the magnetic field configurations and plasma containment strategies needed to achieve net-positive fusion. AI dramatically accelerates the trial-and-error cycle that has historically made fusion research so time-consuming.

## AI in Nuclear Waste Management

Managing nuclear waste — from monitoring storage integrity to planning decommissioning — involves complex spatial reasoning and long-term prediction. AI agents are being applied to:

- **Robotic decommissioning**: AI-guided robotic systems navigate hazardous environments to disassemble reactors with precision
- **Waste characterization**: Computer vision and spectroscopic AI classify waste types for appropriate handling
- **Predictive storage monitoring**: ML models predict corrosion rates and containment integrity over decades

## SMR Design Optimization with AI

Small modular reactors (SMRs) represent a major focus for next-generation nuclear. Their smaller footprint and factory-built modules make them faster to deploy, but they require careful optimization. AI is being used to:

- Optimize passive safety systems that operate without operator intervention
- Improve thermal efficiency through computational fluid dynamics + ML
- Accelerate licensing simulations by training surrogate models

Key players in this space include **Kairos Power**, which uses AI-driven design iteration to reduce costs, and **NuScale Power**, which incorporates digital twins into its SMR operations.

## Key Organizations

- **ITER** (France): International tokamak project using AI for plasma control
- **Commonwealth Fusion Systems** (MIT spinout): AI-optimized high-temperature superconducting magnets
- **Kairos Power**: AI-accelerated SMR design and testing
- **Exxa**: Digital twin AI for existing and next-gen reactors
- **DeepMind + EPFL**: Deep reinforcement learning for fusion plasma control

## Conclusion

AI agents are proving transformative across the nuclear energy landscape. From keeping existing reactors safer to accelerating the path to fusion energy, AI is compressing timelines that once seemed decades away. As SMRs move toward commercial deployment and fusion approaches net energy gain, AI will be indispensable in turning nuclear power's promise into a central pillar of the clean energy transition.
