# Task 2414: Explore AI Agents in Hate Speech Detection, Content Moderation & Online Safety AI

## Overview
Hate speech detection is one of the most deployed and most contested AI applications. Platforms deploy AI to detect and remove harmful speech, but accuracy, bias, and context remain unsolved problems — and the definition of "hate speech" varies enormously across cultures.

## Key Applications
- **Multilingual hate speech detection**: AI detects hate speech across dozens of languages and dialects
- **Contextual moderation**: AI considers conversational context rather than individual words
- **Counter speech generation**: AI generates constructive responses to counter hate narratives
- **Emerging slur tracking**: AI detects newly emerging hate symbols and coded language
- **False positive reduction**: AI distinguishes satire, quotes, and educational content from genuine hate
- **Trend analysis**: AI tracks the geographic and demographic spread of hate movements

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Perspective API (Jigsaw/Google)**: Toxicity scoring API
- **Hive AI**: Multimodal content moderation
- **Spectrum Labs**: Community safety AI
- **Wikipedia ORES**: AI for identifying damaging edits and harassment
- **Jigsaw (Conversation AI)**: Research into better hate speech detection

## Ethical Considerations
- **Cultural relativity**: What counts as hate speech varies radically across cultures
- **False positives**: AI disproportionately flags content from minority communities
- **Language change**: New slang and coded language constantly outpaces AI training
- **Moderation appeals**: No adequate mechanism for users to contest AI decisions
- **Dictator's dilemma**: Hate speech detection tools can be used by authoritarian governments
