# Task 2376: Explore AI Agents in Wildlife Conservation, Endangered Species & Anti-Poaching AI

## Overview
Wildlife conservation is one of the most inspiring applications of AI — protecting elephants, rhinos, tigers, and other endangered species from poaching, habitat loss, and climate change. AI rangers are now watching over wildlife 24/7.

## Key Applications
- **Poaching prevention**: AI monitors camera traps and drone footage to detect poaching activity
- **Species identification**: AI identifies species from photos with higher accuracy than humans
- **Population monitoring**: AI estimates animal populations from camera trap density and distribution
- **Habitat analysis**: AI maps habitat suitability and tracks deforestation from satellite imagery
- **Acoustic monitoring**: AI listens for gunshots, chainsaws, and animal sounds in forests
- **Movement tracking**: AI predicts animal movement patterns to optimize corridor protection

## Tools & Frameworks
- **TrailGuard AI (Intel + Resolve to Protect)**: Anti-poaching camera traps
- **Wildlife Conservation Society AI**: AI for protected area monitoring
- **Wildbook**: AI species identification from photos
- **Rainforest Connection**: AI-powered acoustic monitoring
- **Microsoft AI for Earth (species)**: Wildlife monitoring AI grants
- **Google Biodiversity AI**: Species identification from camera traps

## Ethical Considerations
- **Surveillance creep**: Anti-poaching surveillance may extend to local communities
- **False positives**: AI alerts can disrupt legitimate activities near protected areas
- **Indigenous land rights**: Protected areas must respect indigenous communities' rights
- **Who funds conservation**: AI tools may benefit wealthy conservation organizations over local rangers

## Key Players
Wildlife Conservation Society, TrailGuard AI, Rainforest Connection, Wildbook, Microsoft AI for Earth, Google Conservation
