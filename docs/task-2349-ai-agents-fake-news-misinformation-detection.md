# Task 2349: Explore AI Agents in Fake News, Misinformation Detection & Fact-Checking AI

## Overview
Misinformation is one of the defining threats to democratic societies. AI both creates misinformation (deepfakes, AI-written fake articles) and detects it — an arms race that technology alone cannot win.

## Key Applications
- **Fact-checking AI**: AI verifies claims against trusted databases and sources
- **Deepfake detection**: AI detects AI-generated video and audio impersonations
- **Bot detection**: AI identifies coordinated inauthentic behavior (bots, troll farms)
- **Source credibility scoring**: AI evaluates the reliability of news sources
- **Narrative tracking**: AI maps how stories spread and mutate across social networks
- **Counter-misinformation**: AI generates accurate responses to specific viral false claims

## Tools & Frameworks
- **ClaimBounty / FactCheck.org**: AI-assisted fact-checking
- **Deepware (deepfake detection)**: Open-source deepfake detection
- **Botometer (Indiana University)**: Twitter bot detection
- **Full Fact (UK)**: Real-time fact-checking AI
- **Grover (Allen Institute)**: AI that both generates and detects AI-written text
- **Reality Check (Meta)**: Third-party fact-checking on Facebook

## Ethical Considerations
- **Who watches the fact-checkers?**: Fact-checking organizations have their own biases
- **Chilling effects**: Erroneous misinformation flags may suppress legitimate political speech
- **Scale vs. nuance**: AI can check facts but not contextual truth
- **Deepfake arms race**: Detection AI is perpetually one generation behind generation AI
- **Free speech**: Misinformation prosecution raises serious First Amendment concerns

## Key Players
FactCheck.org, Full Fact, Botometer, Deepware, Allen Institute for AI (Grover), Google Jigsaw, Meta fact-checking
