# Task 2253: Explore AI Agents in Knowledge Management, Semantic Search & Organizational Intelligence AI

## Overview
Organizations accumulate enormous tacit and explicit knowledge that goes unexploited. AI knowledge management systems — semantic search, knowledge graphs, RAG systems — are helping companies and individuals unlock their collective intelligence.

## Key Applications
- **Semantic enterprise search**: AI searches across documents, Slack, email, and databases with natural language understanding
- **Knowledge graph construction**: AI builds interconnected knowledge graphs from unstructured documents
- **RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**: AI grounds responses in an organization's specific documents
- **Expert finding**: AI identifies who in an organization knows what based on communications and documents
- **Policy and procedure synthesis**: AI answers employee questions by synthesizing company policies
- **Lessons learned capture**: AI extracts actionable insights from project post-mortems and meeting notes

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Notion AI / Confluence AI**: AI-powered workspace knowledge bases
- **Glean**: Enterprise semantic search across all workplace apps
- **Mem.ai**: AI-powered personal knowledge management
- **Obsidian (with AI plugins)**: Local-first knowledge graph with AI
- **Microsoft Copilot (Semantic Index)**: M365 integrated knowledge AI
- **Elastic + AI**: Enterprise search with semantic reranking

## Ethical Considerations
- **Knowledge hoarding vs. sharing**: AI may expose knowledge siloed intentionally for political reasons
- **Privacy of communications**: Knowledge management AI may surface private messages shared in "safe" channels
- **Inaccurate synthesis**: AI can confidently synthesize wrong answers from internal documents
- **Intellectual property leakage**: RAG systems could accidentally expose trade secrets

## Key Players
Glean, Notion AI, Mem.ai, Microsoft Copilot, Elastic, Google Workspace AI, Atlassian Intelligence
