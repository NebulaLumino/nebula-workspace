# Task 2313: Explore AI Agents in Genomics, CRISPR & Precision Medicine AI

## Overview
The convergence of AI and genomics is enabling a new era of precision medicine — treatments tailored to your exact genetic code. From AI-designed CRISPR experiments to polygenic risk scores for common diseases, AI + genomics is rewriting medicine.

## Key Applications
- **Variant interpretation**: AI identifies which genetic variants are benign vs. pathogenic (critical for rare disease diagnosis)
- **CRISPR guide design**: AI designs optimal CRISPR guide RNAs to minimize off-target effects
- **Polygenic risk scores**: AI aggregates thousands of genetic variants to predict disease risk
- **Drug target identification**: AI identifies which genes to target for specific diseases
- **Personalized cancer vaccines**: AI designs tumor-specific vaccines from a patient's own tumor genetics
- **Gene expression analysis**: AI reads RNA-seq data to understand cell state and disease

## Tools & Frameworks
- **DeepMind AlphaFold**: Protein structure from sequence (revolutionized structural biology)
- **Benchling (CRISPR design)**: AI-guided CRISPR experiment design
- **Illumina (DRAGEN)**: AI genomics pipeline for sequencing data
- **23andMe (health reports)**: Polygenic risk score AI
- **Genoox**: Clinical genomic variant interpretation
- **Regeneron Genetics Center**: Large-scale human genomics + AI

## Ethical Considerations
- **Genetic privacy**: Your genome is the most personal data — breaches are irreversible
- **Genetic discrimination**: GINA protects against employment/insurance discrimination in the US, but gaps remain
- **Designer babies**: CRISPR + AI could enable selection for non-medical traits
- **Access inequality**: Precision medicine is expensive — benefits may not reach underserved populations
- **Informed consent for data**: Patients rarely understand how their genetic data is used in AI research

## Key Players
DeepMind, Illumina, 23andMe, Regeneron, Benchling, Broad Institute, Genoox
