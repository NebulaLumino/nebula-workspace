# Task 2312: Explore AI Agents in Music Production, Audio Engineering & Songwriting AI

## Overview
AI music production has exploded — from Suno and Udio generating full songs to AI mixing and mastering tools that rival professional engineers. The music industry is grappling with what this means for artists, producers, and listeners.

## Key Applications
- **AI songwriting**: Generate chord progressions, melodies, lyrics, and full arrangements from prompts
- **AI mastering**: Automated mastering with AI that matches commercial loudness and tone
- **AI vocal processing**: Pitch correction, voice synthesis, and vocal layering
- **AI mixing assistance**: Automated mix suggestions, balance adjustments, and effects recommendations
- **Stem separation**: Isolate vocals, drums, bass from mixed tracks (UVR, Demucs)
- **AI music for film/ads**: Generate royalty-free background music for content creators

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Suno AI**: Full song generation from text prompts (most accessible)
- **Udio**: AI music generation with high audio quality
- **LANDR**: AI mastering platform
- **iZotope (RX)**: AI audio repair and enhancement
- **Adobe Podcast**: AI audio enhancement and transcription
- **UVR (Ultimate Vocal Remover)**: Open-source stem separation
- **Meta Demucs**: Open-source music source separation

## Ethical Considerations
- **Copyright of training data**: AI music models trained on copyrighted music without consent
- **Artist displacement**: Session musicians, mix engineers, and mastering engineers face displacement
- **Human artistry debate**: Does AI-generated music have soul? Does it matter?
- **Deepfake music**: AI cloning artists' voices for unauthorized tracks is a growing crisis
- **Royalty structures**: When AI generates music, who gets royalties — the model's creators? The training artists?

## Key Players
Suno, Udio, LANDR, iZotope, Adobe, Meta AI Audio, Google MusicLM, Stability Audio
