# Task 2378: Explore AI Agents in Language Documentation, Endangered Languages & Linguistic Preservation AI

## Overview
Of the ~7,000 languages spoken today, half will be extinct by 2100. AI is being deployed to document, preserve, and even revitalize endangered languages — from generating dictionaries to creating speech synthesizers for languages with no native speakers left.

## Key Applications
- **Speech synthesis for endangered languages**: AI generates pronunciations of languages from written records alone
- **Dictionary and grammar generation**: AI builds dictionaries and grammars from historical texts
- **Language learning apps for heritage learners**: AI creates personalized learning paths for people reconnecting with their heritage language
- **Oral history preservation**: AI transcribes and translates recordings of last native speakers
- **Text digitization**: AI reads handwritten documents in endangered scripts
- **Language revitalization tools**: AI creates flashcards, practice materials, and conversation partners

## Tools & Frameworks
- **ELAN (endangered language archives)**: Documentation tool with AI features
- **Maori language AI (NZ initiatives)**: Active revitalization AI
- **Native American language preservation programs**: Ongoing documentation with AI tools
- **Google's endangered language initiative**: Text-to-speech for rare languages
- **濒危语言 AI projects**: Various academic initiatives
- **UNESCO endangered language monitoring**: International coordination

## Ethical Considerations
- **Whose language data**: Communities must consent to and benefit from AI language tools
- **Cultural ownership**: AI "speaking" a dead language raises questions about cultural appropriation
- **Revitalization vs. documentation**: AI documentation ≠ language revitalization (fluent speakers are needed)
- **Choice to revitalize**: Not all communities want their language revived

## Key Players
UNESCO, WOLD (World Atlas of Language Structures), ELAN, Native communities + universities, Google AI Language
