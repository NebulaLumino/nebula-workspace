# Task 2193: Explore AI Agents in Paralympic Sports & Adaptive Athletics AI

## Overview
Paralympic and adaptive sports have unique equipment, classification, and training needs that AI is particularly well-suited to address — from prosthetic optimization to classification assistance to inclusive training program design.

## Key Applications
- **Classification assistance**: AI helps ensure fair athlete classification by analyzing video and performance data
- **Prosthetic optimization**: AI models optimal prosthetic configuration for running, swimming, or jumping events
- **Wheelchair performance**: AI analyzes wheelchair technique (basketball, racing, tennis) and generates improvement recommendations
- **Inclusive training platforms**: AI generates adaptive training plans for athletes with different physical impairments
- **Equipment customization**: AI helps design and fit adaptive sport equipment (racing chairs, prosthetic legs, ski bindings)

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Oscar Pistorius-era prosthetic research**: Continued advancement in running prosthetic AI optimization
- **Wheelchair motion analysis**: Markerless motion capture adapted for wheelchair athletes
- **Paralympic classification systems**: IPC framework enhanced by data science

## Ethical Considerations
- **Classification gaming**: AI could theoretically be used to game classification systems (deliberately underperforming in one area to qualify for a lower class)
- **Equity of access**: Advanced AI prosthetics and equipment are extremely expensive, creating unfair advantages
- **Privacy of disability data**: Medical/disability data used for classification must be protected

## Key Players
IPC (International Paralympic Committee), Össur (prosthetic manufacturer), Wheelchair & Ambulatory Sports USA, Paralympic sport federations
