# AI Agents in Longevity Science & Aging Research

AI is transforming the science of aging—from measuring biological age to identifying drug targets that could extend healthy human lifespan. These agents work across genomics, proteomics, and clinical data to accelerate discoveries that once took decades.

## Biomarkers & Biological Age Measurement

Traditional aging is measured in chronological years, but AI agents now calculate *biological age* using methylation clocks, blood biomarker panels, and imaging data. Epigenetic clocks like Horvath's DNAm clock, refined by machine learning, predict organ-specific aging rates. Companies like **InsideTracker** use AI to analyze 40+ blood biomarkers and recommend interventions. These systems detect that someone's liver might be "aging" at 60 while their cardiovascular system reads at 45—information impossible to derive from calendar age alone.

## Drug Target Identification for Aging Diseases

Aging itself is increasingly viewed as a treatable condition. AI agents at **Calico** (backed by Google/AbbVie) and **BioAge Labs** analyze molecular pathways of aging to find drug targets. By comparing gene expression in young vs. old tissues, agents identify compounds that could restore youthful function. BioAge's platform has identified IL-11 as a target—blocking this inflammatory protein extended lifespan in mouse studies by 25%.

## Senolytics Research

Senescent cells ("zombie cells") accumulate with age and secrete inflammatory compounds. AI accelerates senolytic drug discovery by modeling which compounds can selectively destroy these cells without harming healthy tissue. **Unity Biotechnology** develops senolytics targeting eye and joint diseases; their Phase 1 trials for膝关节炎 showed meaningful safety profiles. AI agents predict compound toxicity and optimal dosing, shaving years off traditional drug development timelines.

## Wearables & Continuous Health Monitoring

Consumer wearables now run AI models that detect atrial fibrillation, sleep apnea, and early signs of metabolic decline—often before symptoms appear. **Apple Watch's** AI arrhythmia detection and **Whoop's** recovery scoring represent ambient AI agents continuously monitoring physiology. For longevity researchers, this streams billions of real-world data points previously impossible to collect at scale. The integration of continuous glucose monitors with AI prediction models is enabling a new era of personalized metabolic health.

## Epigenetic Reprogramming

The most radical longevity frontier: reverting cells to a younger epigenetic state. **Altos Labs**, backed by Jeff Bezos, pursues cellular rejuvenation using Yamanaka factors—genes that reset cellular age. AI agents model optimal combinations, delivery mechanisms, and partial reprogramming protocols to avoid pushing cells toward cancer. The goal is not immortality but *healthspan extension*: keeping people functional and disease-free for longer.

The convergence of AI with wet-lab biology is compressing what was a 15-year drug development cycle toward 5-7 years. The most significant near-term applications are in biomarker panels (already commercially viable) and drug repurposing, where AI finds new uses for existing compounds. The epigenetic reprogramming frontier remains longer-term but represents perhaps the most transformative possibility: not just treating aging symptoms, but reversing the underlying clock.
