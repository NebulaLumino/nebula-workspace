# Task 2284: Explore AI Agents in Legal Tech, Contract Analysis & Paralegal Automation AI

## Overview
The legal industry is being transformed by AI — from contract review and due diligence to legal research and court opinion analysis. While AI won't replace lawyers entirely, it is automating the routine work that junior associates bill thousands of hours for.

## Key Applications
- **Contract review**: AI reviews contracts for risk clauses, missing provisions, and negotiation points (Kira Systems, LawGeex)
- **Legal research**: AI surfaces relevant case law and statutes faster than manual research (Westlaw Edge, Casetext)
- **Due diligence**: AI automates M&A due diligence review of thousands of documents
- **E-discovery**: AI identifies relevant documents in litigation from massive document sets
- **Legal writing assistance**: AI drafts contracts, briefs, and motions from templates and facts
- **Court opinion analysis**: AI predicts case outcomes based on judge and panel patterns

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Harvey AI**: General-purpose legal AI for law firms (raised $80M+)
- **Casetext (CoCounsel)**: AI legal research and analysis
- **Kira Systems**: Contract review AI
- **LawGeex**: Contract review and approval AI
- **Everlaw**: E-discovery AI platform
- **Ross Intelligence**: Legal research AI
- **Clio (MyCase)**: Law practice management AI

## Ethical Considerations
- **Unauthorized practice of law**: AI providing legal advice vs. information is a blurry line
- **Bias in legal AI**: Training on historical case law encodes historical discrimination into AI outputs
- **Billable hour disruption**: AI threatens the law firm's billable hour model
- **Access to justice gap**: AI could either narrow or widen the civil justice gap for people who can't afford lawyers
- **Confidentiality**: AI training on client data risks attorney-client privilege violations

Harvey AI, Casetext, Kira Systems, LawGeex, Everlaw, Clio, Westlaw (Thomson Reuters), LexisNexis
