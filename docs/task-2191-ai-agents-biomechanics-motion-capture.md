# Task 2191: Explore AI Agents in Biomechanics & Sports Motion Capture AI

## Overview
Biomechanics — the study of human movement — is being transformed by AI-powered motion capture, 3D pose estimation, and force plate analysis. What once required expensive lab equipment and manual expert review now fits in a smartphone app.

## Key Applications
- **AI pose estimation**: MediaPipe, OpenPose, and DeepLabCut extract 3D joint positions from video — no markers needed
- **Force plate + motion integration**: AI synchronizes force plates with video for ground reaction force analysis
- **Injury prediction**: AI identifies movement patterns that precede common injuries (ACL tears, stress fractures, runner's knee)
- **Technique optimization**: Sports-specific AI (swing baseball, swimming stroke, golf) identifies deviations from optimal mechanics
- **Rehabilitation tracking**: AI tracks range-of-motion progress in physio patients over time

## Tools & Frameworks
- **MediaPipe (Google)**: Real-time body/hand/face pose estimation
- **DeepLabCut**: Markerless pose estimation for animal and human movement research
- **The Open Athletic Movement Lab**: Open-source biomechanics analysis pipeline
- **Sports performance tools**: Hudl Technique, Vicon (marker-based), SIMI Motion

## Ethical Considerations
- **Accuracy vs. convenience**: Smartphone pose estimation has lower accuracy than marker-based systems
- **Injury risk communication**: AI injury predictions may cause anxiety or inappropriate activity restriction
- **Coach expertise displacement**: Not all coaches can interpret biomechanical data correctly
