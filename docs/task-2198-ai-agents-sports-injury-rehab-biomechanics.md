# Task 2198: Explore AI Agents in Sports Performance Genetics & Nutrigenomics AI

## Overview
Sports genetics — understanding how individual genetic profiles affect athletic performance, injury risk, and recovery — is one of the most controversial and rapidly evolving areas of sports science. AI is enabling analysis at a scale and speed that was impossible even a decade ago.

## Key Applications
- **ACTN3 gene analysis**: AI analyzes the "speed gene" and predicts sprint vs. endurance athlete potential
- **Injury genetics**: AI identifies genetic variants associated with Achilles tears, ACL injuries, and stress fractures
- **Training response prediction**: AI predicts how individuals respond to endurance vs. power training based on genetics
- **Pharmacogenomics**: AI predicts how individuals metabolize common training aids, anti-inflammatories, and recovery supplements
- **Gene doping detection**: AI flags athletes whose performance improvements are inconsistent with natural training responses
- **Personalized EPO/sports supplement protocols**: AI tailors recovery and performance supplement plans to genetic profiles

## Tools & Frameworks
- **23andMe / AncestryDNA**: Consumer genetic testing with sports-relevant variants
- **Athletigen**: Sports genomics platform
- **Helix (population genomics)**: Large-scale sports genomics research
- **NSCA genetics position statement**: Academic guidelines on genetic testing in sports

## Ethical Considerations
- **Genetic discrimination**: Athletes genetically predisposed to certain injuries may face discrimination from teams/agents
- **Gene doping**: Detection of gene doping (illegal genetic enhancement) is nearly impossible — raises enormous anti-doping challenges
- **Youth athlete screening**: Testing children to predict athletic potential raises serious ethical concerns about limiting opportunity
- **Privacy of genetic data**: Genetic data is the most sensitive personal data — breaches are catastrophic
- **Accuracy limitations**: Most performance-related genetic variants have small effect sizes; over-interpretation is rampant

## Key Players
Athletigen, Helix Sports, 23andMe (sports features), WADA genetics committee, NSCA
