# Task 2383: Explore AI Agents in Autonomous Vehicles, Self-Driving Cars & Transportation AI

## Overview
Autonomous vehicles represent one of the most complex real-world AI deployments — combining computer vision, sensor fusion, path planning, and real-time decision-making at highway speeds. The progress has been uneven; full self-driving remains elusive.

## Key Applications
- **Level 4-5 autonomous driving**: AI handles all driving tasks in defined areas
- **Driver monitoring**: AI detects driver drowsiness and distraction for safety features
- **Traffic optimization**: AI coordinates autonomous vehicles for smoother traffic flow
- **Freight trucking**: Autonomous trucks for long-haul logistics (TuSimple, Waymo Via)
- **Last-mile delivery robots**: Sidewalk robots for local deliveries
- **Autonomous shuttle**: AI shuttles for campus and urban transit

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Waymo (Alphabet)**: Most advanced L4 autonomous (no safety driver in Phoenix)
- **Tesla Autopilot/FSD**: L2+ autonomous driving, controversial for over-reliance
- **Cruise (GM)**: Robotaxi in San Francisco (suspended after incident)
- **Zoox (Amazon)**: Custom autonomous vehicle design
- **TuSimple**: Autonomous trucking
- **Aeva (sensor AI)**: 4D LiDAR for autonomous vehicles

## Ethical Considerations
- **Trolley problem at scale**: Who programs AI to make life-death tradeoffs?
- **Liability in crashes**: When an autonomous car kills someone, who is responsible?
- **Job displacement**: Truck drivers, taxi drivers, delivery drivers face displacement
- **Safety vs. deployment tradeoff**: Rushing deployment vs. waiting for safety proof
- **Surveillance**: AVs create detailed maps of where people travel

## Key Players
Waymo, Tesla, Cruise (GM), Zoox, Argo AI (Ford/VW), TuSimple, Aurora, NVIDIA DRIVE
