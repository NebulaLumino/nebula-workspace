# Task 1745: Explore AI Agents in Language Preservation & Endangered Language AI

## Overview

Of the approximately 7,000 languages spoken worldwide, roughly half are expected to disappear by 2100. Each lost language takes with it unique ways of understanding the world — mathematical concepts, ecological knowledge, oral traditions, and philosophical frameworks that exist in no other tongue. AI agents are emerging as powerful tools in the fight against language extinction.

## The Scale of the Crisis

Languages die when communities stop passing them to children — often due to political pressure, migration, urbanization, or assimilation policies. Once a language has no fluent child speakers, it enters a "sleeping" state. Without intervention, it may be completely extinct within two or three generations.

## How AI Agents Help

**1. Documentation at Scale**
Historically, language documentation required linguists spending years with community speakers. AI agents can accelerate this dramatically:
- Automated speech-to-text for recording sessions
- Pattern recognition to identify grammar rules and morphology
- Glossing and translation assistance
- Cross-referencing with related languages to build etymological trees

**2. Conversational AI in Endangered Languages**
Perhaps the most ambitious application: training conversational AI agents in endangered languages. This requires:
- Voice data collection (sensitive, requires community consent)
- LLM fine-tuning on limited text corpora
- Pronunciation modeling for low-resource languages

The Welsh AI project and Māori language tools are leading examples. Once a conversational agent exists, community members can practice the language even without fluent speakers available.

**3. Pronunciation and Grammar Tutoring**
AI tutors can teach pronunciation, tonal distinctions, and grammatical structures in endangered languages — providing the kind of immersive practice that was traditionally only available through fluent community members.

**4. Story and Oral Tradition Preservation**
Many endangered languages are primarily oral — their literature exists only in the memories of elders. AI agents can help transcribe, translate, annotate, and even generate narrative continuations of oral traditions, creating learnable resources for future speakers.

## Ethical Considerations

**Community consent is paramount.** AI language tools must be developed *with* speaker communities, not *for* them. The linguist's traditional role as an outside documenter is ethically insufficient; community ownership of language data and tools is the emerging standard.

**Data sovereignty**: Languages often carry sacred or culturally restricted content. AI systems must respect access controls set by communities.

**Preservation vs. revival**: Documenting a language as it exists today is different from trying to "revive" it. AI can support both goals, but they require different approaches.

## Leading Projects

- **Welsh (Cy-APL)**: Google's Welsh AI was developed with Welsh government and community partnership
- **Hawaiian**: Papahana Kuaola's language immersion app uses AI tutoring
- **Ojibwe, Cherokee, and Navajo**: Various tribal partnerships with tech companies to build voice assistants
- **ELAN and FLEx**: Software tools used by linguists and communities for corpus building

## Future

The goal isn't just to document endangered languages but to create living speakers. AI agents that can hold conversations, tell stories, teach children, and generate new content in endangered languages represent a profound shift — from museums to vitality.
