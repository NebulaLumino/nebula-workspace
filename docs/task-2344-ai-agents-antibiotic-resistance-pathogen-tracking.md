# Task 2344: Explore AI Agents in Antibiotic Resistance, Pathogen Tracking & Pandemic Prevention AI

## Overview
The antibiotic resistance crisis is projected to kill 10 million people per year by 2050. AI is being deployed across pathogen surveillance, drug discovery for new antibiotics, and outbreak prediction — potentially the most consequential health AI application.

## Key Applications
- **Pathogen genomics surveillance**: AI analyzes genomic sequences of bacteria and viruses to track resistance spread globally
- **Antibiotic discovery**: AI identifies novel antibiotic candidates from massive molecular databases
- **Outbreak prediction**: AI detects early signals of emerging outbreaks from EHR data and wastewater
- **Clinical decision support**: AI helps doctors prescribe the right antibiotic to reduce resistance development
- **Antimicrobial stewardship**: AI monitors and optimizes antibiotic use in hospitals
- **Resistance mechanism prediction**: AI predicts which mutations will confer antibiotic resistance

## Tools & Frameworks
- **PathoGraph (Pathogen genomics)**: Real-time resistance tracking
- **AntibioticAI (drug discovery)**: Novel antibiotic molecule design
- **BlueDot**: First to warn about COVID-19 (January 2020)
- **WHO GLASS (global surveillance)**: WHO antibiotic resistance tracking system
- **BASF / Antabio**: Antibiotic development with AI
- **Cambridge Consultants AI health**: AMR diagnostic AI

## Ethical Considerations
- **Access equity**: New AI-discovered antibiotics may be priced out of reach of the populations that need them most
- **Surveillance vs. privacy**: Pathogen tracking requires genetic data sharing that threatens privacy
- **Dual use**: Antibiotic discovery AI could also be used to design more potent bioweapons
- **Commercial incentives**: Antibiotics are unprofitable — AI won't solve the market failure without policy change

## Key Players
BlueDot, WHO GLASS, PathoGraph, antibiotic AI startups, BARDA (US government), global AMR research networks
