# Task 2380: Explore AI Agents in Human Enhancement, Future Evolution & Biotechnology Ethics AI

## Overview
AI + biotechnology is beginning to blur the line between therapy and enhancement. From CRISPR-based genetic modifications to AI-designed neural implants, the question of what it means to be human is becoming urgent. This is the most speculative and consequential frontier of AI.

## Key Applications
- **AI-designed genetic enhancements**: AI optimizes genetic modifications beyond therapeutic applications
- **Brain-computer interfaces**: Neural implants with AI that augment cognition and memory
- **Longevity biotechnology**: AI-designed interventions to dramatically extend human lifespan
- **AI-controlled pharmaceuticals**: AI designs drugs that alter mood, cognition, and performance
- **Cyborg augmentation**: AI-guided integration of technology into the human body
- **Synthetic biology**: AI designs entirely new organisms — and potentially humans

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Neuralink (Elon Musk)**: Brain-computer interface with AI processing
- **Calico (Google/AbbVie)**: Longevity research AI
- **Altos Labs**: Cellular rejuvenation research with AI
- **Neurotechnology companies**: Various BCI startups (Synchron, Paradromics)
- **Human Genome Editing research**: AI-guided CRISPR applications
- **Transhumanist organizations**: Philosophy meets technology

## Ethical Considerations
- **Access inequality**: Enhancement will initially be for the wealthy — creating a cognitive divide
- **What does human mean?**: Enhancement challenges our philosophical understanding of humanity
- **Coercion by enhancement**: When employers or society expect enhancement, consent becomes pressured
- **Existential risk**: Self-improving AI + human enhancement could produce unpredictable outcomes
- **Religious and cultural opposition**: Enhancement conflicts with beliefs about human nature and dignity

## Key Players
Neuralink, Altos Labs, Calico, Synchron, academic bioethics centers, WHO AI ethics committee
