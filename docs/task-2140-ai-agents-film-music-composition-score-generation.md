# Task 2140: Explore AI Agents in Film Music Composition & Score Generation AI

## Overview
AI music composition for film and television has matured rapidly. From basic cue generation to full orchestral scores, AI is now a genuine tool in the composer's toolkit — used everywhere from Netflix documentaries to indie games to feature films.

## Key Applications

### AI Score Generation
- **Adaptive music systems**: AI generates music that responds to on-screen action in real-time (crucial for games, interactive media)
- **Style replication**: AI trained on a composer's style can generate additional cues in the same vein
- **Orchestration automation**: AI suggests instrumentations and arrangements for MIDI-composed themes
- **Demo/placeholder scores**: AI generates temporary scores for rough cuts, replaced later by live musicians

### Production Tools
- **Hum-to-MIDI**: Composers hum melodies captured via microphone, AI converts to notation
- **Key/tempo detection**: AI automatically detects harmonic content for scoring
- **Dialogue-aware scoring**: AI suggests musical themes that avoid clashing with dialogue frequencies
- **Catalog search**: AI searches royalty-free music libraries by mood, tempo, instrumentation

### Post-Production
- **Stem separation**: AI isolates instruments in mixed tracks for remix or re-orchestration
- **Dynamic mixing**: AI adjusts score mix in real-time based on scene intensity
- **Score-inpainting**: AI fills gaps in incomplete scores

## Tools & Frameworks

### Commercial AI Composition
- **AIVA**: AI composer used for ads, games, and documentaries; SIGNIS label for classical compositions
- **Amper Music (SSM)**: Cloud-based AI music for video/podcasts
- **Soundraw**: AI-generated royalty-free music with customization
- **Boomy**: AI music creation for social media content
- **LANDR**: AI mastering and composition
- **Suno AI / Udio**: AI music generation (general, increasingly used for film)

### Open Source / Research
- **Music Transformer (Google Magenta)**: Attention-based music generation
- **MuseNet (OpenAI)**: Multi-instrumental music generation
- **Transformer-based scoring tools**: Custom fine-tuned models for specific film scores

### Integration
- **Steinberg Cubase / Dorico**: AI-assisted notation and composition plugins
- **Unity FMOD / Wwise**: Adaptive game audio (AI-driven middleware)

## Ethical Considerations
- **Composer displacement**: Documentary composers, trailer music composers, and indie game composers face immediate economic threat
- **Copyright of training data**: AI models trained on copyrighted film scores (John Williams, Hans Zimmer) raise IP questions
- **Creative homogenization**: AI models optimized for "average" preference may flatten distinctive musical voices
- **Credit and royalty ambiguity**: Who owns the copyright to AI-generated music? Who gets residuals?
- **Aesthetic mediocrity**: AI scores may converge toward "safe" compositions that don't take risks

## Future Outlook
AI won't replace John Williams — but it will replace the team of composers who write stock music for industrial films, training videos, and documentary B-roll. The film music industry will stratify: legendary composers remain irreplaceable, but mid-tier scoring work shifts to AI-human hybrid workflows. The most exciting frontier is truly adaptive film scores that re-compose themselves in real-time based on viewer emotional engagement.
