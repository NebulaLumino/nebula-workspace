# AI Agents in Nuclear Energy Communication & Public Perception

## Overview

Nuclear energy is one of the most politically contentious topics in energy policy — and one of the most potent tools for decarbonization. Nuclear power produces virtually no carbon emissions, operates at very high capacity factors (typically 90%+), and can provide the reliable baseload power that intermittent renewables struggle to match. Yet public fear stemming from accidents like Chernobyl and Fukushima, concerns about radioactive waste, and associations with nuclear weapons have created powerful opposition movements. AI agents are now being deployed to understand, measure, and shift the nuclear narrative — from public communication to policy advocacy.

## How AI Agents Are Applied

**Public Perception Analysis & Sentiment Tracking:** AI agents continuously monitor social media, news articles, public comments on energy policy, and community meeting transcripts to map nuclear sentiment in real time. They can identify the specific concerns driving opposition in a given community (waste? safety? cost? equity?), track how sentiment shifts following media events, and segment populations by attitude — from "nuclearphobes" to "nuclearphiles." This granular understanding allows communicators to tailor messages to specific audiences rather than deploying generic reassurance.

**Narrative Strategy & Message Optimization:** AI agents can generate and test nuclear communication messages at scale — drafting op-eds, social media campaigns, FAQ documents, and community presentation talking points optimized for different audiences (environmentalists, conservatives, local governments, Indigenous communities). Agents can also model which framings are most effective: Does leading with climate benefits change minds? Does emphasizing modern reactor safety records outperform historical comparisons? A/B testing these narratives in focus groups and online experiments allows agents to refine strategies empirically.

**Countering Disinformation:** AI agents monitor for nuclear disinformation campaigns — whether from fossil fuel interests, anti-nuclear advocacy groups, or foreign state actors amplifying nuclear safety fears. Agents can detect coordinated inauthentic behavior, fact-check nuclear misinformation in near-real-time, and generate corrected content for distribution through trusted channels. This "nuclear communication hygiene" is increasingly important as social media amplifies fear-based narratives.

**Technical Communication & Transparency:** Nuclear communication faces a unique challenge: the technical complexity of radiation risk, waste management, and reactor safety is far beyond what most publics have the scientific background to independently evaluate. AI agents can translate technical nuclear information into accessible, accurate, non-alarmist language — serving as a bridge between expert knowledge and public understanding. They can also generate plain-English summaries of safety reports, environmental impact statements, and regulatory filings that make them genuinely accessible.

**Stakeholder Engagement Facilitation:** AI agents can support the complex stakeholder engagement processes required for nuclear facility siting and relicensing. They can synthesize community input, identify areas of consensus and contention, draft compromise language, and help facilitate productive dialogue between nuclear operators, regulators, and host communities. Agents can also help design community benefit agreements that address local concerns about safety and economic impacts.

**Policy Analysis & Advocacy:** AI agents analyze energy policy proposals, modeling the carbon reduction implications of different nuclear scenarios — What if all proposed nuclear plants are built? What if existing plants are retired early? — and communicating findings to policymakers, journalists, and advocacy organizations. Agents can also track legislative developments across jurisdictions, identify opportunities for pro-nuclear policy advocacy, and draft model legislation.

## Key Technologies & Datasets

NLP models analyze social media, news, and public comment data at scale. Sentiment analysis and topic modeling identify the key concerns driving nuclear opposition. Simplex and agent-based models simulate public opinion dynamics and how information propagates through social networks. Integration with energy system models (like SAM, OSeMOSYS) allows agents to ground their communications in quantitative decarbonization analysis.

## Challenges & Considerations

AI-generated nuclear communication faces serious ethical considerations. An AI agent optimized purely for persuasion — without regard to transparency — could produce manipulative messaging that undermines informed public deliberation. The line between "informing the public" and "spinning the public" is thin. There's also the risk that AI agents trained to defend nuclear power could minimize legitimate safety concerns or suppress valid criticism. Any nuclear communication AI system needs to be designed with epistemic honesty as a core constraint, not a variable to optimize.

## Outlook

The most sophisticated nuclear communication AI systems will combine persuasion science with genuine transparency — helping publics engage with nuclear energy's genuine risks and genuine benefits on their merits. As advanced reactors (small modular reactors, molten salt reactors) move from demonstration to deployment, AI agents will be essential for communicating the safety and environmental advantages of these new technologies. The nuclear renaissance — if it happens — will be, in part, an AI-mediated conversation.
