# Task 2166: Explore AI Agents in Food Waste Reduction & Upcycling AI

## Overview
Roughly 30-40% of all food produced is wasted. AI is tackling food waste at every point in the supply chain — from farm to fork to landfill — by predicting waste, optimizing portion sizes, and transforming waste into value-added products.

## Key Applications

### Supply Chain Waste Reduction
- **Demand forecasting at retail**: AI reduces overordering by predicting exact demand for perishable goods
- **Cold chain monitoring**: IoT sensors + AI detect temperature excursions that cause spoilage before products arrive
- **Dynamic pricing for near-expiry food**: AI triggers automatic discounts as food approaches its best-by date
- **Farm surplus redistribution**: AI platforms (like Too Good To Go) match surplus food to consumers

### Consumer-Focused Waste Reduction
- **Smart fridge inventory**: Computer vision tracks fridge contents and warns of approaching waste
- **Recipe generation from leftovers**: AI generates meals from whatever ingredients are in the fridge
- **Portion planning**: AI calculates exact portions needed for households or events to avoid over-preparation

### Upcycling & Waste Valorization
- **Waste stream analysis**: AI identifies what agricultural and food processing waste can be upcycled (spent grain → protein bars, fruit peels → antioxidants)
- **Upcycling recipe generation**: AI generates products from waste streams — upcycled flour from imperfect produce, snack bars from brewers' spent grain
- **Molecule extraction planning**: AI identifies valuable compounds in food waste for nutraceutical or cosmetic applications

## Tools & Frameworks

### Commercial
- **Too Good To Go**: Surplus food marketplace with AI matching
- **FoodMinds**: Upcycling ingredient sourcing and formulation
- **Winnow**: AI food waste tracking for commercial kitchens
- **Agrisoma**: Carinata/smogina cover crop upcycling into aviation fuel
- **Impossible Foods / Beyond Meat**: Upcycling waste agricultural products into protein

### Open Source / Research
- **USFDA Food Waste databases**: Open data on food waste composition
- **Circular food systems research**: Academic work on waste valorization pathways
- **AgriPredict AI**: Crop failure and waste prediction

## Ethical Considerations
- **Greenwashing**: Some "upcycled" products are marketed as sustainable with minimal actual waste reduction
- **Food vs. fuel vs. feed**: Upcycling waste into fuel may compete with food uses in times of scarcity
- **Access equity**: Surplus food redistribution primarily benefits urban areas; rural food waste is harder to redirect
- **Regulatory gaps**: Upcycled ingredients lack clear regulatory pathways in many markets

## Future Outlook
AI-driven food waste reduction could cut global food waste by 30% by 2035 — equivalent to feeding 1 billion more people. Upcycling food waste into high-value products (nutraceuticals, bioplastics, animal feed) is becoming economically viable as AI identifies the most valuable compounds. The biggest opportunity is in the home kitchen: AI-guided "cook what's in your fridge" could be the single biggest consumer waste reduction tool.
