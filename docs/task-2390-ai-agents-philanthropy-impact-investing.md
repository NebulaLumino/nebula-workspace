# Task 2390: Explore AI Agents in Philanthropy, Impact Investing & Grantmaking AI

## Overview
Philanthropy and impact investing represent the positive application of AI — directing resources toward social good. AI is being used to optimize charitable giving, measure impact, and identify the highest-leverage interventions.

## Key Applications
- **Cause prioritization**: AI identifies which social problems offer the highest marginal impact per dollar
- **Grant matching**: AI matches funders to organizations and vice versa
- **Impact measurement**: AI helps nonprofits and funders measure and report social outcomes
- **Effective altruism tools**: AI supports data-driven charitable decision-making
- **Corporate social responsibility**: AI helps companies optimize CSR portfolios
- **Disaster relief allocation**: AI directs disaster donation funds to highest-need organizations

## Tools & Frameworks
- **GiveWell (AI research)**: AI-assisted research into charitable effectiveness
- **Open Philanthropy**: AI-informed grantmaking for maximum impact
- **ImpactMatters**: AI-powered nonprofit impact ratings
- **Candid (GuideStar)**: AI for nonprofit data and grant discovery
- **ImpactAlpha**: AI-driven impact investing news and deal flow
- **GlobalGiving (AI matching)**: AI matches donors to vetted nonprofits

## Ethical Considerations
- **Quantification bias**: AI optimization tends to fund what can be measured, not what matters most
- **Colonialism in giving**: AI effective altruism may impose Western-defined priorities globally
- **Gaming the metrics**: Organizations optimize for what AI measures, not genuine impact
- **Concentration of power**: AI tools in philanthropy concentrate power in the hands of tool-builders
- **Counterfactual neglect**: It's hard to know what would have happened without your intervention

## Key Players
GiveWell, Open Philanthropy, ImpactMatters, Candid, GlobalGiving, EA Funds, major foundation AI initiatives (Gates Foundation)
