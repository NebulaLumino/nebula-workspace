# Task 2345: Explore AI Agents in Social Media Moderation, Content Policy & Hate Speech Detection AI

## Overview
Social media platforms moderate billions of posts using AI — with enormous consequences for free speech, public discourse, and the spread of misinformation. The accuracy and bias of these systems is one of the most contested areas in technology.

## Key Applications
- **Toxicity detection**: NLP models detect hate speech, harassment, and harmful content at scale
- **Contextual moderation**: AI considers conversation context to distinguish satire from genuine hate speech
- **Multimodal moderation**: AI analyzes images, video, audio, and text together for coordinated campaigns
- **Evasion detection**: AI detects intentionally obscured hate symbols and coded language
- **Appeals processing**: AI helps users appeal content moderation decisions
- **Proactive policy detection**: AI identifies emerging harmful trends before they go viral

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Meta's AI Moderation**: Largest-scale content moderation in the world
- **Perspective API (Jigsaw/Google)**: Toxicity scoring API
- **Hive AI**: Multimodal content moderation
- **Spectrum Labs**: Community safety AI for user-generated content platforms
- **ActiveFence**: Trust and safety AI for platforms
- **Adobe Content Authenticity**: AI provenance tracking for images

## Ethical Considerations
- **Free speech vs. harm**: Moderation thresholds are inherently subjective and culturally relative
- **Algorithmic bias**: Hate speech classifiers perform worse on African American Vernacular English and minority dialects
- **Transparency**: Neither users nor researchers can audit how moderation actually works
- **Global vs. local norms**: One-size-fits-all moderation fails across cultures
- **Amplification vs. suppression**: Moderation shapes what ideas spread — without democratic input

## Key Players
Meta AI, Jigsaw (Google), Hive AI, Spectrum Labs, ActiveFence, Adobe, Foundation for Press Freedom
