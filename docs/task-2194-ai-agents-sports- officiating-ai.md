# Task 2194: Explore AI Agents in Sports Officiating & Referee Assistance AI

## Overview
AI is being integrated into sports officiating at an accelerating pace — from Hawk-Eye ball tracking in tennis to VAR in football to automated umpire strike zones in baseball. The technology is mature; the ethical debates are just beginning.

## Key Applications
- **Ball tracking**: Hawk-Eye, TrackMan, and similar systems for ball trajectory and out-of-bounds calls
- **Automated umpiring**: Statcast/TrackMan for ball/strike calls; automated soccer offside line
- **VAR decision support**: AI flags incidents for human referee review (fouls, handballs, red cards)
- **Gaming the system detection**: AI detects simulation (diving) and feigning injury
- **Fraud detection**: AI spots match-fixing patterns in officials' decision-making data
- **Rule interpretation consistency**: AI applies rulebook consistently where human referees vary

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Hawk-Eye (Sony)**: Ball tracking for tennis, cricket, football (VAR)
- **TrackMan**: Baseball, golf, and missile tracking radar + AI
- **FIFA VAR**: AI-assisted video operation room for soccer
- **MLB Robot Umpires (ABS)**: Automated ball/strike system
- **EyeTrackGPT**: Attention modeling for sports officiating research

## Ethical Considerations
- **Human vs. machine accountability**: Who is responsible when an AI makes a wrong call?
- **Pace of game**: AI reviews slow down the game — when does precision outweigh flow?
- **Referee job displacement**: AI umpires threaten professional officiating careers
- **System failures**: Network failures, calibration errors, and edge cases — who catches them?
- **Accuracy standards**: "95% accurate" still means 1 in 20 calls wrong — is that acceptable?

## Key Players
Hawk-Eye (Sony), TrackMan, FIFA (VAR), MLB (ABS), Opta (sports data), Genius Sports
