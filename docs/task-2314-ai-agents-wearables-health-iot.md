# Task 2314: Explore AI Agents in Wearables, Health IoT & Continuous Monitoring AI

## Overview
The Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) — wearable ECG, continuous glucose monitors, smart inhalers, and patch monitors — generates continuous health data. AI is the only practical way to make sense of this firehose, enabling early detection and personalized intervention.

## Key Applications
- **Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM)**: AI analyzes glucose patterns to optimize diet, exercise, and insulin for diabetics
- **Wearable ECG/AFib detection**: Apple Watch and others use AI to detect atrial fibrillation from wrist signals
- **Sleep apnea detection**: AI detects sleep apnea from overnight oximetry and accelerometer data
- **Fall detection and elderly monitoring**: AI alerts caregivers when falls occur or patterns change
- **Smart inhaler adherence**: AI tracks when inhalers are used and predicts exacerbations for asthma/COPD patients
- **Medication adherence AI**: AI monitors pill bottles and smart packs to detect missed doses

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Dexcom G7/G6 (CGM)**: Real-time glucose AI for diabetes management
- **Apple Watch (AFib detection)**: FDA-cleared AI arrhythmia detection
- **Whoop**: Strain/recovery AI platform with respiratory rate detection
- **Withings (health IoT)**: Connected scales, BP monitors, sleep mats
- **Propeller Health (smart inhaler)**: Asthma/COPD adherence AI
- **Eli Health**: Hormone monitoring via smart devices

## Ethical Considerations
- **False alarm burden**: Constant monitoring generates false positives that cause anxiety and medical visits
- **Data privacy**: Continuous health data is enormously sensitive and profitable
- **Insurance discrimination**: Wearable data could be used to deny life/long-term care insurance
- **Monitoring without consent**: Employers or family members monitoring someone without their full understanding
- **Clinical validation gap**: Many wearables have limited clinical evidence for their AI claims
