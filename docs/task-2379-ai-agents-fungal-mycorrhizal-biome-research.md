# Task 2379: Explore AI Agents in Fungal Intelligence, Mycorrhizal Networks & Ecosystem AI

## Overview
The "wood wide web" — mycorrhizal fungal networks connecting trees — is one of nature's most fascinating systems. AI is enabling scientists to map, model, and understand fungal networks at unprecedented scale, with implications for forestry, agriculture, and climate.

## Key Applications
- **Fungal network mapping**: AI analyzes soil samples and isotopic data to map fungal networks
- **Tree communication modeling**: AI models how trees share nutrients through fungal networks
- **Forest health monitoring**: AI detects early signs of forest disease from satellite and soil data
- **Symbiosis prediction**: AI predicts which fungal-plant pairings will optimize forest regeneration
- **Carbon sequestration modeling**: AI models how fungal networks affect soil carbon storage
- **Mushroom identification**: AI identifies wild mushrooms from photos with high accuracy

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Suzanne Simard ( mycorrhizal research)**: The "wood wide web" researcher now using AI
- **Fungal Planet AI**: Emerging fungal identification tools
- **Forest soil AI**: Carbon and fungal mapping research
- **Merlin (mushroom AI)**: Fungal identification apps
- **Soil microbiome AI**: Academic research on fungal-plant interactions

## Ethical Considerations
- **Exploitation of traditional knowledge**: Indigenous peoples have long understood fungal networks — AI projects should credit this
- **Commercialization of wild fungi**: AI helping identify rare mushrooms could accelerate overharvesting
- **Intentional introduction risk**: AI-guided fungal introduction to ecosystems could have unintended consequences
- **Ecological balance**: Understanding fungal networks doesn't mean we should manipulate them
