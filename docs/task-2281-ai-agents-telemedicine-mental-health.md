# Task 2281: Explore AI Agents in Telemedicine, Telehealth & Mental Health App AI

## Overview
Telehealth exploded during COVID-19 and has permanently changed healthcare access. AI is now central to telehealth platforms — from AI symptom checkers and triage systems to real-time mental health support chatbots. The quality and ethics of these systems vary enormously.

## Key Applications
- **AI symptom triage**: Chat-based systems assess symptoms and route to appropriate care level (NHS 111 AI, Ada Health)
- **Mental health chatbots**: AI companions like Woebot, Wysa, and Replika provide 24/7 CBT-based support
- **Remote patient monitoring**: AI analyzes data from connected devices (blood pressure, glucose, wearables) between appointments
- **AI-assisted diagnosis**: AI reviews patient data and suggests diagnoses for physician review
- **Administrative automation**: AI handles appointment scheduling, notes, and billing in telehealth
- **Crisis detection**: AI monitors app interactions for suicidal ideation and triggers human intervention

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Ada Health**: AI symptom assessment used by NHS and health systems
- **Woebot Health**: CBT chatbot for depression and anxiety
- **Wysa**: AI-powered mental health chatbot
- **Infermedica**: AI triage and symptom assessment API
- **Babylon Health (folded)**: AI telemedicine platform ( cautionary tale of overpromising)
- **K Health**: AI primary care triage

## Ethical Considerations
- **Emergency situations**: AI chatbots have failed to escalate real emergencies in time
- **Data privacy**: Intimate health conversations with AI raise data security concerns
- **Clinical liability**: Who is liable when AI gives bad medical advice?
- **Access equity**: Telehealth + AI may widen access gaps for those without smartphones/internet
- **Dependence risk**: Vulnerable people may prefer AI to human care, delaying real treatment

Ada Health, Woebot, Wysa, Infermedica, K Health, Teladoc, Babylon Health alumni
