# Task 2318: Explore AI Agents in End-of-Life Care, Palliative Medicine & Death Doula AI

## Overview
Death is the most universal human experience — and one of the least technologically addressed. AI is beginning to enter end-of-life care, from palliative care decision aids to death doula chatbots, raising profound questions about what a "good death" means.

## Key Applications
- **Palliative care decision aids**: AI helps patients and families navigate complex end-of-life decisions (feeding tubes, ventilation, hospice)
- **Death doula chatbots**: AI companions for dying people and their loved ones — providing presence, reflection, and logistical help
- **Advance care planning**: AI helps people create and update living wills and POLST forms
- **Grief support AI**: AI chatbots provide grief support after loss (like Replika for bereavement)
- **Funeral planning AI**: AI helps navigate funeral planning, estate organization, and death administrative tasks
- **Legacy preservation**: AI helps create legacy documents, memory books, and video messages for dying people

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Cake (end-of-life planning)**: AI-assisted advance care planning
- **Gathering Sunshine (bereavement)**: Grief support AI
- **Everplan (estate planning)**: AI for end-of-life document organization
- **Aware Healthcare (palliative AI)**: AI-assisted palliative care
- **Death doula networks**: Human + AI hybrid support models
- **Reflective AI**: AI systems designed to have meaningful end-of-life conversations

## Ethical Considerations
- **Who is responsible when AI gives bad end-of-life advice?**
- **Replacing human presence**: Is an AI death doula a meaningful form of companionship?
- **Cultural diversity**: End-of-life practices vary enormously across cultures — AI must not impose a single model
- **Access inequality**: Good death requires resources — AI for the wealthy, nothing for the poor
- **Artificial hope**: AI may offer false hope or avoid necessary conversations about mortality
- **The dead and their data**: What happens to AI systems trained on a dead person's communications?

## Key Players
Cake, Everplans, Palliative Care AI research, Death doula associations, End-of-Life University
