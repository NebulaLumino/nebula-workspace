# Task 2222: Explore AI Agents in Smart Fashion, Wearable Tech & e-Textiles

**Status**: ✅ Complete

## Overview
The intersection of fashion and technology — smart fabrics, biometric clothing, LED-integrated garments — is a frontier where AI is enabling new forms of self-expression, health monitoring, and interactive fashion.

## Key Applications
- **Biometric fashion**: Clothing with integrated sensors (heart rate, posture, temperature) that respond to the wearer's body
- **Color-changing fabrics**: AI-controlled e-ink and thermochromic fabrics that change color based on mood or context
- **Fitness tracking clothing**: AI processing data from fabric-embedded sensors to provide real-time coaching
- **LED/fiber integration**: Wearable light-up fashion with AI-generated patterns
- **AR try-on**: AI + AR enabling virtual garment overlay on live video
- **Mood-responsive clothing**: Fabric that changes texture or appearance based on biometric signals interpreted by AI

## Tools & Frameworks
- **Levi's Commuter x Jacquard**: Smart jacket with Google Jacquard threads
- **Under Armour HOVR**: Running shoes with integrated AI fitness tracking
- **Ralph Lauren Polo Tech**: Biometric smart shirts
- **Augmentscape**: AR fashion try-on
- **CuteCircuit**: LED-integrated smart fashion

## Ethical Considerations
- **Privacy of biometric data**: Smart clothing collects intimate body data — who owns and profits from it?
- **Accessibility**: High-tech clothing may exclude people with disabilities who use certain assistive devices
- **Planned obsolescence**: Tech-integrated clothing becomes e-waste faster than conventional fashion
- **Surveillance**: Smart clothing could potentially track wearers' movements and biometrics without consent

## Key Players
Levi's/Google Jacquard, Under Armour, Ralph Lauren, CuteCircuit, Augmentscape, Sensoria
